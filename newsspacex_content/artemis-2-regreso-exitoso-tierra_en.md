# Artemis 2 Splashes Down Successfully, Raising Stakes for the Lunar Program

On April 10, the Artemis 2 Orion capsule performed a textbook splashdown in the Pacific Ocean, capping off an epic ten-day crewed trip around the Moon. The crew returned safe, hauling back an impressive payload of lunar photography.

While NASA views the flight as an unmitigated success, the subsequent schedule constraints heavily pressure SpaceX. The accelerating Orion cadence puts the spotlight on delays for SpaceX's HLS lander, forcing Musk's company to drastically speed up crucial orbital propellant transfer demonstrations.
