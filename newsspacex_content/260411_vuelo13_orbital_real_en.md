# Flight 13: The First Real Orbital Flight in Starship's History

SpaceX has confirmed a massive paradigm shift for Starship's Flight 13. Unlike previous tests, this flight will not be suborbital. Leaked FCC permits reveal that Ship 40 has explicit authorization to reach and maintain a real Earth orbit.

Standing 52 meters tall with approximately one thousand tons of mass, Starship will become the heaviest object ever placed in orbit in a single launch, doubling the mass of the International Space Station. The mission profile includes orbital insertion, a cruise period, and a controlled deorbit with a splashdown in the Pacific.
