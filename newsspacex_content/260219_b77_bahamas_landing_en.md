# Historic Landing of Falcon 9 Booster B77 (B1077) in the Bahamas

On **February 19, 2026**, SpaceX marked another milestone in its relentless pursuit of operational efficiency with the successful launch and recovery of the **Falcon 9 B1077** booster. This mission, the 26th for this veteran vehicle, featured a precision landing in Bahamian waters.

## Mission and Landing Details
The flight lifted off from Space Launch Complex 40 (**SLC-40**) at Cape Canaveral, carrying a payload of **29 Starlink satellites** to low Earth orbit.

*   **Reusability Milestone:** With this flight, B1077 completed its **26th mission**, having previously participated in key flights such as Crew-5, CRS-28, and multiple Starlink launches. This cadence demonstrates the maturity of SpaceX's reusability technology.
*   **Landing Location:** Following stage separation, the booster performed a vertical landing on the autonomous droneship **"Just Read the Instructions"**, strategically positioned in **Exuma Sound, The Bahamas**.
*   **Second Time in The Bahamas:** This is the second occasion a Falcon 9 booster has landed in Bahamian waters, following the reopening of this recovery corridor by the Civil Aviation Authority of The Bahamas.
*   **Trajectory Optimization:** Landing at this location allows for advanced optimization of flight trajectories, maximizing payload capacity for specific Starlink missions.

## Regional Impact
The booster's return was accompanied by **sonic booms** audible across various parts of the islands, a physical reminder of the aerospace activity now regularly occurring over the Caribbean. This regulatory clearance is a significant vote of confidence in the statistical reliability of SpaceX's systems.
