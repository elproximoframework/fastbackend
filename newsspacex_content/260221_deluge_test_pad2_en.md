# Massive Deluge System Test at Orbital Launch Pad 2 (OLP-2)

SpaceX has achieved a critical milestone in Starbase's ground infrastructure development with a full-scale test of the water deluge system at **Orbital Launch Pad 2 (OLP-2)**. This infrastructure is essential for supporting the devastating thrust of the 33 Raptor 3 engines on the Super Heavy booster.

## Technical Details of the Test
The test, conducted on **February 16, 2026**, validated the advanced design of the flame deflector and the acoustic energy suppression systems.

*   **Deflector Geometry:** Unlike Pad 1, the trench at Pad 2 channels gases at an angle of **30 to 45 degrees** to the south. This design redirects kinetic and thermal energy smoothly, protecting the concrete and improving tracking camera visibility by reducing the spherical steam cloud.
*   **Water Capacity:** The system handled approximately **350,000 gallons** of water during activation, simulating the thermal conditions of a real liftoff.
*   **Closed-Loop Recycling System:** One of the most notable innovations is the water recycling system using integrated sump pumps in the trench, allowing for quick recovery and reuse for successive tests.
*   **DSS (Detonation Suppression System):** The system injecting a mixture of water and nitrogen gas to displace oxygen and prevent methane explosions under the mount was successfully tested.

## Raptor 3 Engine Context
This validation sets the stage for Booster 19, which will be the first flight vehicle to use 33 **Raptor 3** engines. Each engine generates approximately 280 metric tons of thrust, totaling over 9,000 tons at liftoff (three times the power of the Saturn V).

The new Raptor 3 architecture eliminates the need for external heat shields thanks to its internal regenerative cooling, drastically simplifying maintenance and system reliability.
