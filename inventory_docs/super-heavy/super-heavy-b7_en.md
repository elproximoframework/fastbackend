## History and Mission (IFT-1)

Booster 7 is historically significant as the first Super Heavy booster to perform an integrated launch of the Starship system. Its development was extremely complex, including an accidental explosion on the pad during a spin-prime test in July 2022 that required major repairs.

On February 9, 2023, B7 performed the most powerful static fire in history, igniting 31 of its 33 engines simultaneously. Finally, on April 20, 2023, it lifted off from Starbase alongside Ship 24. During ascent, multiple engines failed prematurely, and the hydraulic steering system (HPU) was compromised by fires in the engine bay.

Despite reaching an altitude of 39 km, the vehicle lost control after failing to achieve stage separation, leading to the activation of the Flight Termination System (FTS). B7 exploded over the Gulf of Mexico, marking the end of a mission that, although ending in destruction, provided critical data for the redesign of all future iterations.

---

## Innovations and Legacy

- **33-Engine Ignition:** Successfully validated the control system's ability to manage the sequential ignition of a massive fleet of cryogenic engines.
- **Ascent Resilience:** Demonstrated that the stainless steel structure could survive a liftoff and ascent even with multiple engine failures.
- **Pad Redesign:** Its launch significantly damaged the launch pad (due to the lack of a water deluge system), which directly led to the implementation of the "steel plate" and water deluge sound suppression system for all subsequent flights.
