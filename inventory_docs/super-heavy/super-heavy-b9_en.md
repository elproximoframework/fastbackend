## History and Mission (IFT-2)

Booster 9 introduced massive changes based on the lessons from IFT-1. The most radical shift was the elimination of hydraulic systems for Thrust Vector Control (TVC), replacing them with an all-**electrical** system, which reduced the risk of fires and complex mechanical failures. Additionally, it incorporated a "hot-staging" separation ring for the first time.

On November 18, 2023, B9 lifted off successfully, with all 33 Raptor 2 engines remaining ignited throughout the entire ascent. Following the hot-staging maneuver (where S25 ignited its engines while still attached to the booster), B9 initiated its boostback turn. However, during the boostback burn, multiple engines failed in a chain reaction due to fuel filter clogging caused by propellant slosh, resulting in the vehicle's controlled explosion via the FTS.

---

## Innovations and Legacy

- **Electric TVC Real-World Test:** Proved that the electrical steering system is robust and reliable under launch loads.
- **Hot-Staging Validation:** Demonstrated that the booster could survive the direct ignition of the upper stage engines thanks to the protective top heat shield.
- **Raptor 2 Reliability:** Achieved the historic milestone of maintaining 33 engines working perfectly throughout the initial ascent phase.
