# Super Heavy Booster 8 (B8) — Structural and Testing Pathfinder

**Type:** Ground Test Article  
**Serial Number:** B8 (Booster 8)  
**Status:** Retired / Scrapped  
**Purpose:** Structural load and fatigue testing  

---

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Height | ~71 m |
| Diameter | 9 m |
| Engines | Not equipped for flight |
| Final Status | Dismantled in 2023 |
| Key Role | Validation of second-generation components |

---

## History

Booster 8 was manufactured almost simultaneously with Booster 7, but it was never intended for launch. While the program focused on solving B7's ignition and control issues, B8 served as a build-site workhorse to validate minor structural improvements.

This booster was crucial for testing fuel transfer systems (venting and pressurization) that would later be implemented into more advanced models. As a close structural twin to B7, it allowed engineers to perform "proof-loading" (maximum load) tests without risking the primary flight hardware. Eventually, after being superseded by the Booster 9 design (which introduced radical changes), B8 was retired and posteriormente dismantled to free up space at Starbase.

---

## Legacy

- **Strategic Redundancy:** Acted as a constant test bed during the months leading up to IFT-1.
- **Materials Validation:** Used to study the degradation of stainless steel after multiple cryogenic pressurization cycles on the ground.
