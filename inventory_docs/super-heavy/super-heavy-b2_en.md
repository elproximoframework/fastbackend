# Super Heavy Booster 2 (B2) — Production Test Prototype

**Type:** Ground Test Article  
**Serial Number:** B2 (Booster 2)  
**Status:** Retired / Scrapped  
**Engines:** Not equipped for flight  

---

## Technical Specifications

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Height | ~70 m |
| Diameter | 9 m |
| Engines | Not installed (Pathfinder) |
| Key Role | Validation of ring welding techniques |
| Final Status | Dismantled in 2021 |

---

## History

Booster 2 was one of the first full-scale prototypes following the initial success of the Starship (SN series) hops. Unlike B1, which was a stacking pathfinder, B2 was used to refine the robotic welding techniques for the 9-meter diameter stainless steel rings.

While it never underwent intense cryogenic testing like its successors, B2 was vital for Starbase engineers to learn how to handle manufacturing tolerances for such a massive structure. Its sections were later scrapped or reused as training pieces for new assembly technicians.

---

## Legacy

- **Manufacturing Maturity:** Allowed the transition from hand-built prototypes to more automated processes.
- **Logistics Validation:** Served to test heavy booster transport equipment within the Starbase construction area.
