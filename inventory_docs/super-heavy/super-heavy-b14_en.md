## History and Innovations

Booster 14 marks the beginning of the **Block 2** era for SpaceX's super-heavy launcher. The most obvious change is its increased height, achieved by adding an additional stainless steel ring. This stretch allows for an increase in propellant capacity by about 300 metric tons, providing the extra thrust required to launch the Starship V2 spacecraft, which are also taller and heavier.

In addition to the size increase, B14 introduces improvements in the internal distribution of methane and liquid oxygen plumbing, optimizing flow for the Raptor 3 engines that will fully debut in later units. B14 serves as the technical bridge between the fast-learning prototypes (Block 1) and the high-payload operational vehicles (Block 2) that will form the backbone of the Artemis lunar missions.

---

## Key Improvements

- **Stretched Chassis:** Increased tank volume for long-duration missions or massive payloads.
- **Flight Electronics V2:** More compact and vibration-resistant avionics systems.
- **Raptor 3 Readiness:** While capable of flying with Raptor 2, its structure is prepared for the higher chamber pressures of the next-generation engine.
