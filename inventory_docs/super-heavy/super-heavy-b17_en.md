## History

Booster 17 was the last unit manufactured under the strict standards of the Block 2 (V2) design. Originally intended for a test flight in late 2025, the rapid progress of the **Starship V3 (Block 3)** architecture rendered B17 technically "obsolete" before its launch.

SpaceX made the strategic decision not to fly B17 to focus all launch pad resources on the new generation of more powerful vehicles. In November 2025, after completing a series of cryogenic pressure tests that confirmed the strength of the stretched chassis, B17 was retired and scrapped. Its engines and critical components were reused at the McGregor test stand and in new V3 units, marking the official end of the Block 2 development phase.

---

## Legacy

- **Closing the V2 Cycle:** Served as the final validation that the stretched stainless steel design was structurally sound.
- **Transition Efficiency:** Its scrapping demonstrated SpaceX's philosophy of prioritizing technological progress over the simple use of existing hardware.
