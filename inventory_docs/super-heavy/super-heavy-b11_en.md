## History and Mission (IFT-4)

Booster 11 marked a critical milestone on the path to full reusability. Following the landing burn failures of previous missions, SpaceX focused on ensuring B11 could perform a stable descent and a precise final burn over water.

On June 6, 2024, B11 successfully completed its ascent and hot-staging phases. During reentry, it executed a perfect boostback burn and, for the first time, achieved a successful landing burn over the waters of the Gulf of Mexico. The vehicle remained vertical and stable for several seconds before tipping over into the ocean, validating that all control and propulsion systems were ready for a launch tower capture attempt.

---

## Innovations and Legacy

- **Splashdown Validation:** It was the first Super Heavy booster to survive intact until water contact in a controlled position.
- **Refining Reentry:** Telemetry data confirmed that fuel filtration and gas management systems definitively resolved the engine cavitation issues.
- **Green Light for Mechazilla:** The success of B11 gave SpaceX the necessary confidence to attempt catching the next booster (B12) with the tower arms.
