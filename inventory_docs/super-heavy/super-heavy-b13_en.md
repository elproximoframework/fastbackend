## History and Mission (IFT-6)

Booster 13 flew only a month after the success of B12, with the goal of demonstrating catch repeatability. However, during descent, telemetry systems detected a minor anomaly in the launch tower's health criteria. Following safety protocols, SpaceX aborted the catch attempt and directed B13 to a controlled splashdown in the Gulf of Mexico.

Despite not being caught, B13 performed a flawless descent, igniting its landing engines and gently touching down in the ocean before exploding after tipping over. This mission was vital for testing contingency maneuvers and the flight software's ability to make critical, millisecond decisions to protect ground infrastructure.

---

## Innovations and Legacy

- **Safety Protocols:** Demonstrated the effectiveness of autonomous safety systems in preventing damage to the Mechazilla tower.
- **Operational Redundancy:** Validated that the system can switch between catching and splashdown without losing control of the vehicle.
- **Preparation for Block 2:** It was one of the last "Block 1" design boosters to fly, closing a phase of accelerated learning.
