## History and Mission (IFT-3)

Booster 10 flew in one of the most successful Starship missions to date. Following the propellant slosh and filter clogging issues observed in B9, SpaceX implemented critical improvements in the tank headers and fuel filtration systems to prevent propellant movement from affecting the engines during return maneuvers.

On March 14, 2024, B10 lifted off perfectly, maintaining all its engines throughout the ascent phase. It achieved a flawless hot-staging maneuver with S28 and, for the first time, completed nearly the entire boostback burn. However, during the supersonic descent toward the Gulf of Mexico, the vehicle experienced attitude control issues (excessive roll) that prevented the final landing burn. B10 broke apart at approximately 462 meters above sea level, but it provided invaluable data regarding the reentry phase.

---

## Innovations and Legacy

- **Propellant System Reliability:** Demonstrated that the tank design corrections solved the massive engine loss problem during boostback that plagued B9.
- **Perfected Hot-Staging:** Solidified this separation method as the standard for the entire Starship system.
- **Recovery Readiness:** Was the first booster to demonstrate a nearly complete controlled return arc, clearing the way for the capture attempt with the tower arms in subsequent missions.
