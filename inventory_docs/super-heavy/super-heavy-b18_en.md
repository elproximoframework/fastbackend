## History

Booster 18 was the first full-scale vehicle built with the **Starship V3** architecture. This design introduced profound changes in the fuel tank structure and the base heat shield to accommodate the increased power of the Raptor 3 engines.

During a critical cryogenic pressure test in November 2025, B18 suffered a catastrophic structural failure in the methane tank. Although the vehicle was destroyed, the incident was considered a "learning success" by SpaceX. The data collected identified unforeseen stress points caused by the new internal stiffener ring design, leading to immediate manufacturing changes in Booster 19 and Booster 20 to prevent similar failures.

---

## Innovations and Legacy

- **V3 Validation:** It was the first physical hardware to test the limits of the new V3 chassis.
- **Cryo-Testing Lessons:** Identified critical vulnerabilities in the automated welding process of the new high-pressure tanks.
- **Accelerated Evolution:** Its sacrifice allowed subsequent units (B19+) to be significantly more robust.
