# Super Heavy Booster 3 (B3) — The First to "Roar"

**Type:** Cryogenic and Static Test Prototype  
**Serial Number:** B3 (Booster 3)  
**Status:** Retired / Scrapped (August 2021)  
**Engines:** 3 × Raptor 1 (Static Fire tested)  
**Key Date:** First Static Fire (July 19, 2021)

## History

Booster 3 made history on July 13, 2021, by becoming the first Super Heavy prototype to successfully complete a full-duration cryogenic pressure test. It was taken to Suborbital Pad B, where it was loaded with liquid nitrogen to validate the structural integrity of its tanks under simulated launch conditions.

Just a few days later, on July 19, 2021, B3 performed the first-ever static fire of a Super Heavy rocket, igniting three Raptor 1 engines simultaneously. Although it was a brief burn, it proved the design's capability to handle the immense power of multiple engines. B3 never flew, as its purpose was to validate systems before the launch of B4. It was scrapped a month after its tests.

---

## Innovations and Legacy

- **First Cryogenic Validation:** Confirmed that SpaceX's stainless steel could withstand the pressures of a 70-meter booster.
- **Propulsion Milestone:** Performed the first engine ignition in the history of the Super Heavy family.
- **Pad Preparation:** Served to calibrate the fuel supply systems of the suborbital area for booster operations.
