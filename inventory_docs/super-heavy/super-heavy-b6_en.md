## History

Booster 6 was a vital piece in the Starship program's transition toward orbital flight vehicles. Manufactured in 2021, its construction overlapped with the famous Booster 4 and its successor, Booster 7. Unlike them, B6 was never intended to be equipped with all 33 Raptor engines for a launch.

Its primary purpose was to serve as a static test bed at the Starbase build site. Through B6, SpaceX validated improvements in the robotic manufacturing processes of the stainless steel rings and tested the integrity of the elliptical domes under extreme pressure. As a "pathfinder," it allowed the engineering team to identify potential structural failure points before committing the expensive Raptor 2 engines to Booster 7.

Eventually, after fulfilling its test cycle and becoming obsolete due to rapid design iterations (which led directly to the B7 and B9 configurations), B6 was dismantled in 2022 to make way for more advanced hardware.

---

## Legacy

- **Design Qualification:** Validated the structural changes needed to withstand the dynamic loads of an orbital launch.
- **Welding Efficiency:** Allowed for the refinement of longitudinal and circumferential welding processes in the Starbase High Bay.
- **Preparation for B7:** Many of the lessons learned from the controlled failures of B6 were applied directly to Booster 7, the first vehicle to fly in the integrated program.
