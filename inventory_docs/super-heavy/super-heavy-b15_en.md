## History and Innovations

Booster 15 represents the culmination of the fully operational **Block 2** design. It is the first booster to integrate **Raptor 3** engines as standard, which completely eliminate individual external thermal shielding, drastically simplifying the inspection and maintenance process following a catch.

In its maiden flight (early 2025), B15 demonstrated that the stretched design and new engines are capable of even greater landing precision than their predecessors. The successful catch of B15 by the Mechazilla arms confirmed the maturity of the Block 2 system, allowing SpaceX to transition from a "flight test" phase to a "frequent launch operations" phase. With this unit, the Starship program enters its industrial phase, capable of putting massive Starlink V3 constellations and supplies for Lunar Base Alpha into orbit.

---

## Key Improvements

- **Final Raptor 3 Engines:** Increased thrust and lower dry weight thanks to the removal of superfluous external components.
- **Base Thermal Protection:** New simplified aft shield that is more resistant to reentry plasma.
- **Operational Efficiency:** Reduction in turnaround time (preparation between flights) from weeks to days.
