## History and Mission (IFT-5)

Booster 12 was the star of what many consider the most impressive milestone in modern aerospace engineering history. On October 13, 2024, after lifting off and launching Ship S30 into a suborbital trajectory, B12 returned toward Starbase with unprecedented precision.

After re-entering the atmosphere and performing an extremely aggressive three-engine landing burn, B12 approached the "Mechazilla" launch tower. In a maneuver choreographed to the millimeter, the booster hovered as the tower's "Chopstick" arms closed around its mounting points. For the first time in history, a 71-meter-long rocket with hundreds of tons of mass was "caught" in mid-air before touching the ground, enabling theoretically immediate reusability.

---

## Innovations and Legacy

- **The Catch:** Validated Elon Musk's vision of eliminating landing legs to reduce weight and allow for rapid re-launches.
- **Navigational Precision:** The booster landed with a deviation of only a few centimeters from the central target.
- **Reusability Milestone:** Made Starship the first super-heavy transport system with the capacity for full-precision return to the launch pad.
