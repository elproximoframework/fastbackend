## History

Booster 5 was assembled shortly after B4 with the intention of being the second vehicle capable of performing an orbital flight. However, its operational lifespan was extremely short. As SpaceX refined the Booster 7 design (introducing support for Raptor 2), B5 was quickly abandoned.

Unlike B4, which spent months in the Rocket Garden, B5 was moved to the Mega Bay shortly after its basic structure was completed. There, it was scrapped in 2022 to free up space for the mass production of more advanced boosters. Its existence primarily served to refine the workflow on the Starbase assembly line before the design was standardized in subsequent series.

---

## Legacy

- **Rapid Iteration:** Exemplifies SpaceX's philosophy of discarding entire hardware units in favor of a significantly superior architecture.
- **Flow Optimization:** Helped identify bottlenecks in the manufacturing of large cylindrical structures within the Mega Bay.
