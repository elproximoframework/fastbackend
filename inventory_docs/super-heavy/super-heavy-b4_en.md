## History

Booster 4 is perhaps the most iconic prototype of the early Starbase era. It was the first booster designed to reach orbit and became famous for being stacked for the first time with Starship SN20 in August 2021, creating the tallest rocket ever built at the time (~120 meters).

Despite undergoing multiple stacking and test sequences on the Orbital Launch Mount (OLM), it never flew. The rapid development of the Raptor 2 engine and structural changes implemented in Booster 7 made B4 obsolete. It remained in the Starbase "Rocket Garden" for over 600 days as a visual reminder of the program's progress until it was finally moved to the Mega Bay and scrapped in March 2024 to make way for newer iterations.

---

## Innovations and Legacy

- **First Full Stacking:** Validated the vertical assembly procedures for the complete Starship system.
- **Propulsion Scalability:** Was the first to integrate 29 Raptor 1 engines, testing the fuel distribution systems for a massive fleet.
- **Starbase Icon:** Its presence on the pad for months provided critical data on the marine corrosion resistance of the stainless steel structure.
