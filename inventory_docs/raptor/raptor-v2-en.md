# Raptor 2 — Power and Refined Simplicity

**Type:** Liquid Methane / LOX rocket engine  
**Version:** Raptor 2  
**Cycle:** Full-flow staged combustion (FFSC)  
**Production Facility:** SpaceX McGregor / Brownsville  
**First Firing:** Late 2021  
**Status:** Operational — Main engine for Starship and Super Heavy (Block 1)  

---

## Technical Specifications

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Sea Level Thrust | ~230 tf (2,256 kN) |
| Vacuum Thrust (RVA2) | ~258 tf (2,530 kN) |
| Sea Level Specific Impulse (ISP) | ~327 s |
| Vacuum Specific Impulse (ISP) | ~350-380 s (depending on version) |
| Chamber Pressure (target) | 300 bar |
| Engine Mass | ~1,600 kg |
| Fuel | CH₄ — Liquid Methane |
| Oxidizer | LOX — Liquid Oxygen |
| Thermodynamic Cycle | Full-flow staged combustion (FFSC) |
| Throttling Range | 40–100% |

## History

### Evolution from Raptor 1 to Raptor 2
Following successful testing with Raptor 1 (V1), SpaceX identified that the original design, while revolutionary, was extremely complex to manufacture at scale. Raptor 1 was internally known for its "Christmas tree" of external piping, sensors, and actuators.

**Raptor 2** was a radical redesign focused on **simplicity, power, and manufacturability**. SpaceX removed much of the peripheral instrumentation, simplified propellant routing, and replaced many flange connections with permanent welds. This approach not only reduced the engine mass by approximately 400-500 kg but also allowed for an increase in combustion chamber pressure from 250 bar to 300 bar, resulting in a thrust increase from 185 tf to 230 tf.

### Implementation in Starship
Raptor 2 became the standard for orbital flight tests (IFT - Integrated Flight Tests). The first integrated flight, IFT-1 in April 2023, exclusively used Raptor 2 engines on both the Super Heavy (Booster 7) and the Starship (Ship 24).

Throughout 2023 and 2024, Raptor 2 has demonstrated significantly higher reliability compared to initial prototypes, enabling milestones such as the successful ignition of all 33 Booster engines and the controlled reentry of the Starship stage.

---

## Innovations and Improvements

- **Design Simplicity:** The removal of external components drastically reduced manufacturing time and potential failure points.
- **Improved Thrust-to-Weight Ratio:** Thanks to mass reduction and increased thrust, Raptor 2 offers one of the best thrust-to-weight ratios in the world for an engine of its class.
- **Higher Chamber Pressure:** Achieving and maintaining 300 bar of chamber pressure stably is a technical milestone that maximizes the efficiency of the FFSC cycle.
- **Integrated Ignition:** Ignition systems were improved to be more robust and less dependent on complex external support systems.
- **Paving the Way for Raptor 3:** Raptor 2 served as the necessary technological bridge for the development of Raptor 3, which further eliminates external thermal shielding by integrating cooling channels deeper into the engine structure.
