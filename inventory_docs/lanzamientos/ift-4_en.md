# IFT-4 — Starship Fourth Integrated Flight Test

**Launch Date:** June 6, 2024  
**Location:** Starbase, Boca Chica, Texas  
**Vehicles:** Super Heavy Booster 11 + Starship Ship 29  
**Result:** MISSION SUCCESS — Soft splashdown of both stages

## History

IFT-4, launched on June 6, 2024, marked the greatest success of the Starship program to date. The primary objective was to demonstrate the ability of both stages to survive their return maneuvers and perform controlled, soft splashdowns.

Booster 11 performed a perfect ascent. Following separation, it executed its Boostback Burn and, for the first time in the Super Heavy program, successfully completed its landing burn, touching down softly and vertically on the waters of the Gulf of Mexico before tipping over, as planned.

Ship 29 continued its flight on a suborbital trajectory. The most dramatic moment occurred during reentry. Onboard cameras showed extreme heat melting part of one of the forward flaps and damaging the heat shield. Despite these visible structural damages in real-time, the ship maintained attitude control.

Ship 29 survived the extreme temperatures of reentry and executed its "flip" maneuver and final landing burn, achieving a soft splashdown in the Indian Ocean. This milestone validated the thermal protection system design and the redundancy of flight controls.

---

## Innovations and Lessons Learned

- **Booster Recovery:** The Super Heavy's soft splashdown proved that the vehicle is structurally capable of precision landings.
- **Reentry Survival:** Ship 29 demonstrated exceptional resilience to extreme heat, even with significant damage to its side flaps.
- **Navigation Software:** Improvements in control algorithms allowed the ship to stabilize despite the partial loss of aerodynamic surfaces.
- **Path to Catching:** This success paved the way for the next big challenge: attempting to catch the booster with the "Mechazilla" tower arms.
