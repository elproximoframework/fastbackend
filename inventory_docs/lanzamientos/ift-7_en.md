# IFT-7 — Starship Seventh Integrated Flight Test

**Launch Date:** January 16, 2025  
**Location:** Starbase, Boca Chica, Texas  
**Vehicles:** Super Heavy Booster 14 + Starship Ship 33  
**Result:** Partial Success — Second Booster catch; Ship lost during ascent  

---

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Booster Engines | 33 × Raptor 2 |

| Maximum altitude reached | ~150 km (pre-failure) |
| Flight duration | ~8.5 minutes |
| Stage separation | Successful (Hot Staging) |

---

## History

IFT-7 launched on January 16, 2025, continuing SpaceX's rapid testing pace. The mission's primary goals were to consolidate booster recovery techniques and advance the reliability of the upper stage's ascent.

Booster 14 executed a flawless flight. After separation, it returned precisely to Starbase, where it was caught for the second time in history by the tower's "chopstick" arms. This success proved that the catch achieved during IFT-5 was not a fluke but a repeatable and robust process.

However, Ship 33 faced difficulties shortly after separation. At approximately T+8:30, a fire was detected in the ship's aft section during the insertion burn. This led to a loss of structural integrity and a Rapid Unscheduled Disassembly (RUD) before reaching orbit. Subsequent investigations revealed that unanticipated vibrations damaged critical propulsion system components.

---

## Innovations and Lessons Learned

- **Catch Consolidation:** The second successful catch validated control algorithms and the tower's mechanical resilience for consecutive operations.
- **Vibration Analysis:** Data from Ship 33 helped identify previously undetected harmonics affecting fuel lines under peak load.
- **Iteration Cycle:** Despite the loss of the ship, the rapid booster recovery allowed SpaceX to focus resources on correcting upper-stage issues.
