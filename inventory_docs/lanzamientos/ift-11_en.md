# IFT-11 — Starship Eleventh Integrated Flight Test

**Launch Date:** October 13, 2025  
**Location:** Starbase, Boca Chica, Texas  
**Vehicles:** Super Heavy Booster 15 + Starship Ship 38  
**Result:** Full Success — Final Block 2 flight; successful deployment and extreme reentry testing  

---

## Technical Specifications

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Booster Engines | 33 × Raptor 2 |
| Ship Version | Final Block 2 (Ship 38) |
| Payload | 8 Starlink simulators (Deployed) |
| Maximum altitude reached | ~192 km |
| Flight duration (Ship) | ~66 minutes |
| Booster Outcome | Controlled splashdown (Gulf of Mexico) |

---

## History

IFT-11, launched on October 13, 2025, marked the end of an era for SpaceX: it was the final flight of the Starship Block 2 configuration and the first-generation Super Heavy. The goal was to collect final performance data before transitioning to the next generation of hardware.

Booster 15 performed a unique test during its return, utilizing a 13-engine landing burn configuration (later transitioning to 5 and finally to 3). This maneuver, designed for future, higher-mass boosters, was successfully executed before the booster performed a soft splashdown in the Gulf of Mexico.

Ship 38 completed an extended mission of over an hour. During the orbital phase, it successfully deployed its payload simulators and performed a precision engine relight. For reentry, SpaceX deliberately removed several heat shield tiles to "stress" the structure and find the limits of the steel's endurance. Despite these extreme tests, the ship survived reentry, executed its flip maneuver, and performed a soft splashdown in the Indian Ocean.

---

## Innovations and Lessons Learned

- **Thermal Stress Testing:** The tile removal confirmed that Starship's stainless steel structure can withstand partial thermal shield failures without catastrophic loss.
- **13-Engine Configuration:** The success of the 13-engine booster burn validated the propulsion architecture for the next generation of Super Heavy (Block 3).
- **Operational Consolidation:** With IFT-11, SpaceX concluded the initial development phase, demonstrating that the system is capable of performing complex missions repeatedly.
