# IFT-6 — Starship Sixth Integrated Flight Test

**Launch Date:** November 19, 2024  
**Location:** Starbase, Boca Chica, Texas  
**Vehicles:** Super Heavy Booster 13 + Starship Ship 31  
**Result:** Success — Booster splashdown and in-space Ship relight  

---

## Technical Specifications

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Booster Engines | 33 × Raptor 2 |
| Total liftoff thrust | ~7,590 tonforce |
| Maximum altitude reached | ~228 km (Post-relight) |
| Flight duration | 1h 05m 24s |
| Stage separation | Successful (Hot Staging) |

---

## History

IFT-6 launched on November 19, 2024, with the objective of pushing Starship's operational limits. While a booster catch was not repeated for safety reasons, the mission achieved several critical objectives for the program's future.

Booster 13 performed a nominal ascent, but during descent, real-time tower monitoring systems detected that not all safety criteria were met for a catch attempt. Consequently, the booster was diverted to perform a soft splashdown in the Gulf of Mexico, which it successfully executed.

Ship 31 reached space and performed the first-ever relight of a Raptor engine in vacuum conditions. This capability is essential for future deorbit maneuvers and long-duration orbital missions. Additionally, the ship conducted a much more aggressive reentry with a steeper angle of attack to test the structural and thermal resilience of the flaps under extreme conditions.

Finally, Ship 31 performed a soft splashdown in the Indian Ocean during daylight, providing spectacular images of the final landing maneuver.

---

## Innovations and Lessons Learned

- **In-Space Relight:** The successful Raptor ignition in a vacuum validated ignition and propellant management systems outside the atmosphere.
- **Catch Abort Criteria:** Demonstrated the robustness of autonomous safety systems that decide between capture or splashdown at the last moment.
- **Reentry Limits:** The flight provided valuable data on how Starship manages extreme thermal loads during more direct descents.
- **Flap Testing:** Intentional removal of tile sections in critical areas allowed for studying the safety margins of the flap's base material.
