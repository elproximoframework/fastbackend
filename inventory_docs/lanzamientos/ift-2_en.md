# IFT-2 — Starship Second Integrated Flight Test

**Launch Date:** November 18, 2023  
**Location:** Starbase, Boca Chica, Texas  
**Vehicles:** Super Heavy Booster 9 + Starship Ship 25  
**Result:** Partial Success — Booster RUD post-separation and Ship RUD in space  

---

## Technical Specifications

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Booster Engines | 33 × Raptor 2 |

| Flight duration | ~485 seconds |
| Stage separation | Successful (Hot Staging) |

## History

IFT-2 (Integrated Flight Test 2) marked a massive technological leap following the lessons learned from IFT-1. The launch took place on November 18, 2023, from the OLP-1 platform at Starbase. Unlike the first flight, all 33 Raptor engines on Booster 9 ignited correctly and remained operational throughout the initial ascent phase.

The mission's most critical milestone was the implementation of "Hot Staging." At approximately T+2:41, Ship 25 ignited its engines while still attached to Booster 9, utilizing a new vented ring to protect the top of the booster. The separation was flawless, marking the first time a vehicle of this scale executed this maneuver.

Following separation, Booster 9 initiated its flip maneuver and "Boostback Burn" to return toward the Texas coast. However, shortly after starting the burn, several engines failed, and the booster experienced a Rapid Unscheduled Disassembly (RUD). SpaceX attributed this to a filter blockage in the liquid oxygen supply system.

Meanwhile, Ship 25 continued its ascent, becoming the first Starship to reach outer space by surpassing the 100 km altitude mark (Kármán Line). The ship reached a maximum altitude of ~149 km and a velocity near 24,000 km/h. Near the end of its scheduled burn (T+8:05), a liquid oxygen leak occurred during a venting maneuver, leading to a fire and the activation of the Autonomous Flight Safety System (AFSS), resulting in the loss of the vehicle.

---

## Innovations and Lessons Learned

- **Engine Reliability:** The successful ignition of all 33 Raptor engines validated improvements to the fuel system and design robustness.
- **Hot Staging:** Demonstrated the feasibility of hot staging, allowing for increased payload capacity by maintaining thrust during transition.
- **Pad Protection:** The new steel plate and water deluge system performed perfectly, leaving the OLM virtually intact after liftoff.
- **Space Reach:** Validated Starship's behavior in the vacuum of space and the performance of thermal tiles under real flight conditions.
