# IFT-10 — Starship Tenth Integrated Flight Test

**Launch Date:** August 26, 2025  
**Location:** Starbase, Boca Chica, Texas  
**Vehicles:** Super Heavy Booster + Starship Ship  
**Result:** Full Success — First successful payload deployment; controlled reentry and soft splashdown  

---

## Technical Specifications

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Booster Engines | 33 × Raptor 2 |
| Payload | 8 Starlink simulators (Successfully deployed) |
| In-space Relight | Successful (Raptor engine) |
| Reentry | Successful (Thermal protection intact) |
| Splashdown | Soft (Indian Ocean) |

---

## History

IFT-10, conducted on August 26, 2025, is considered the first fully successful test flight of the Starship system as a whole. Following the lessons from IFT-9, SpaceX implemented critical hardware improvements in the payload and propulsion systems.

The ascent was nominal. After separation, the Super Heavy performed a controlled descent and a soft splashdown in the Gulf of Mexico (no catch was attempted on this mission to focus resources on the upper stage).

The true feat occurred in space: for the first time, Starship successfully deployed 8 Starlink satellite simulators, demonstrating its payload system's operational readiness. Subsequently, a successful in-space relight of a Raptor engine was performed to adjust the reentry trajectory. The ship survived the intense heat of reentry, executed its flip maneuver, and performed a soft splashdown in the Indian Ocean, meeting all mission objectives.

---

## Innovations and Highlights

- **Payload Readiness:** The successful deployment paved the way for definitive operational missions for the Starlink network.
- **Reentry Reliability:** Improvements to the heat shield allowed the ship to reach sea level with minimal thermal damage.
- **Propellant Management:** The in-space relight validated the fuel logistics necessary for future lunar and interplanetary missions.
