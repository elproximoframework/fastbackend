# IFT-1 — Starship First Integrated Flight Test

**Launch Date:** April 20, 2023  
**Location:** Starbase, Boca Chica, Texas  
**Vehicles:** Super Heavy Booster 7 + Starship Ship 24  
**Result:** Failure — Rapid Unscheduled Disassembly (RUD) at ~T+3:59  

---

## Technical Specifications

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Booster Engines | 33 × Raptor 2 |
| Total liftoff thrust | ~7,590 tonforce |
| Maximum altitude reached | ~39 km |
| Flight duration | ~239 seconds |
| Stage separation | Not achieved |

## History

IFT-1 (Integrated Flight Test 1) was the first attempt to fly the complete Starship system as a fully integrated two-stage rocket. On April 17, 2023, a first launch attempt was aborted due to a frozen pressurization valve on Booster 7.

On April 20, 2023, at 8:33 CDT, the vehicle successfully lifted off from Orbital Launch Pad 1 (OLP-1) at Starbase. This milestone made Starship the most powerful rocket ever flown, surpassing the Saturn V from the Apollo era.

However, the flight experienced multiple anomalies from the outset. Three of the 33 Raptor 2 engines on Booster 7 failed at liftoff, with additional engine failures occurring during ascent. At approximately T+85 seconds, the vehicle lost thrust vector control (TVC) on the 13 central cluster engines, making trajectory control impossible.

Without steering capability, the vehicle began to rotate uncontrollably, surpassing the intended stage separation angle. As the required conditions for the Hot Staging maneuver were not met, the Autonomous Flight Safety System (AFSS) was activated. The vehicle experienced a Rapid Unscheduled Disassembly (RUD) at approximately T+3:59, over the Gulf of Mexico, after receiving the flight termination command.

Additionally, the launch pad suffered severe structural damage. The absence of a sound suppression system (Water Deluge) allowed acoustic forces and hot gas flow to destroy part of the foundation beneath the Orbital Launch Mount (OLM), launching concrete and soil fragments into the air — an event informally dubbed "rocket lavarocks."

---

## Innovations and Lessons Learned

- **First full integration:** This was the first flight of the complete Starship system, testing the mechanical and electrical integration of both stages.
- **33 Raptor 2 engines:** Broke the historical thrust record at liftoff, validating that the stack could lift its own weight.
- **Critical flight data:** Despite the failure, SpaceX gathered invaluable telemetry data on aerodynamic behavior, engine performance under dynamic loads, and AFSS operation.
- **Resulting improvements:** Analysis of the failure led to the design of the "Hot Staging Ring" for IFT-2 onward, implementation of the Fire Suppression System (FSS/Deluge) on the launch pad, and flight software updates for greater engine control redundancy.
