# IFT-8 — Starship Eighth Integrated Flight Test

**Launch Date:** March 6, 2025  
**Location:** Starbase, Boca Chica, Texas  
**Vehicles:** Super Heavy Booster 15 + Starship Ship 34  
**Result:** Partial Success — Third Booster catch; Ship lost during ascent  

---

## Technical Specifications

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Booster Engines | 33 × Raptor 2 |
| Total liftoff thrust | ~7,590 tonforce |
| Maximum altitude reached | ~160 km (pre-failure) |
| Flight duration | ~9.5 minutes |
| Stage separation | Successful (Hot Staging) |

---

## History

IFT-8 took place on March 6, 2025. SpaceX aimed to apply corrections from the IFT-7 vibration failure to finally achieve full orbital insertion for Starship.

Booster 15 demonstrated exceptional reliability, completing its flight and achieving the third consecutive catch at the tower. This consistency in booster recovery marked the beginning of the operational phase for this technology, allowing for detailed inspections and faster refurbishment.

Ship 34 featured improved structural reinforcements and vibration dampers. It managed to surpass its predecessor's flight time, reaching approximately 9.5 minutes of flight. Unfortunately, a secondary navigation system failure caused a trajectory deviation that forced the activation of the AFSS, resulting in a RUD before the scheduled engine cutoff.

---

## Technical Innovations

- **Recovery Routine:** With three successful catches, SpaceX began standardizing the Super Heavy recovery process, reducing the time between missions.
- **Booster Maturity:** The design of Booster 15 was considered mature enough to start focusing optimization on mass reduction and operating costs.
- **New Navigation Challenges:** The failure of Ship 34 highlighted the need for greater redundancy in guidance and control systems during the final orbital insertion phase.
