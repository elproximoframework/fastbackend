# IFT-5 — Starship Fifth Integrated Flight Test

**Launch Date:** October 13, 2024  
**Location:** Starbase, Boca Chica, Texas  
**Vehicles:** Super Heavy Booster 12 + Starship Ship 30  
**Result:** HISTORIC SUCCESS — First-ever Booster catch at the tower

## History

IFT-5, launched on October 13, 2024, will go down in astronautical history for achieving an unprecedented technical feat: the catch of Super Heavy Booster 12 by the launch tower arms (Mechazilla).

Following a perfect ascent and separation, Booster 12 executed its return maneuver toward Starbase. As it approached the tower, the vehicle performed its landing burn with millimeter precision. The tower's "chopstick" arms closed around the booster in mid-air, securing it before it could touch the ground. This milestone is fundamental to SpaceX's vision of rapid and total reusability.

Ship 30, meanwhile, continued its trajectory toward the Indian Ocean. The ship demonstrated significant improvements in its thermal shield, incorporating new, more resistant tiles and secondary protection layers. Ship 30 survived reentry with minimal damage and performed a smooth and precise splashdown in the ocean waters, culminating the mission in total success.

---

## Innovations and Lessons Learned

- **Tower Catch:** Validated the viability of a legless landing system, reducing booster weight and simplifying recovery infrastructure.
- **Navigation Precision:** Booster control during final descent demonstrated the centimeter-level precision required for docking with the tower arms.
- **Advanced Heat Shield:** Ship 30 showed far less degradation than Ship 29, validating changes in materials and joint design.
- **Ground Infrastructure:** The tower and its mechanical systems proved their capability to withstand the dynamic loads and heat of a direct landing.
