# IFT-3 — Starship Third Integrated Flight Test

**Launch Date:** March 14, 2024  
**Location:** Starbase, Boca Chica, Texas  
**Vehicles:** Super Heavy Booster 10 + Starship Ship 28  
**Result:** Partial Success — Orbital velocity achieved; Ship lost during reentry  

---

## Technical Specifications

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Booster Engines | 33 × Raptor 2 |
| Total liftoff thrust | ~7,590 tonforce |
| Maximum altitude reached | ~234 km |
| Flight duration | ~49 minutes |
| Stage separation | Successful (Hot Staging) |

---

## History

IFT-3, launched on March 14, 2024, was a mission of historic milestones. For the first time, Starship achieved its planned trajectory and the velocity required for orbital insertion (though a closed orbit was not intended). Booster 10 performed perfectly during ascent, executing a flawless Hot Staging maneuver.

Following separation, Booster 10 successfully executed its Boostback Burn, but during the final landing burn attempt over the Gulf of Mexico, several engines failed, and contact was lost at approximately 462 meters altitude, resulting in a RUD.

Once in space, Ship 28 conducted critical tests: opening and closing the payload door (Pez Door) and a large-scale propellant transfer demonstration between internal tanks—a key requirement for NASA's Artemis program. However, a vacuum Raptor engine relight was not attempted due to excessive vehicle roll.

During atmospheric reentry, Ship 28 provided the first real-time images of plasma surrounding the vehicle thanks to the Starlink network. Unfortunately, the ship lost attitude control and disintegrated at approximately 65 km altitude over the Indian Ocean.

---

## Innovations and Lessons Learned

- **Orbital Velocity:** Demonstrated that Starship has the power required to reach Low Earth Orbit (LEO).
- **In-Space Operations:** The payload door test and propellant transfer confirmed essential operational concepts.
- **Starlink Telemetry:** Using Starlink terminals allowed for maintained connection during the reentry plasma phase, a first in the industry.
- **Thermal Shield Data:** Analysis of the flight data helped identify weaknesses in the heat shield tile mounting.
