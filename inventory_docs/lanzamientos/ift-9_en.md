# IFT-9 — Starship Ninth Integrated Flight Test

**Launch Date:** May 27, 2025  
**Location:** Starbase, Boca Chica, Texas  
**Vehicles:** Super Heavy Booster (Refitted from IFT-7) + Starship Ship  
**Result:** Partial Success — First booster reflight; payload deployment failure and ship lost during reentry  

---

## Technical Specifications

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Booster Engines | 33 × Raptor 2 |
| Reflight | First time (Booster from IFT-7) |
| Payload | 8 Starlink simulators |
| Maximum altitude reached | Orbital |
| Booster Outcome | Lost during landing burn |

---

## History

IFT-9, launched on May 27, 2025, marked a historic milestone: the first time a Super Heavy Booster flew for a second time. Having been caught during IFT-7, the booster was refurbished and returned to the pad, demonstrating the system's potential for rapid reuse.

Ascent and stage separation performed correctly. However, during its return, the booster was lost in the Gulf of Mexico due to a loss of telemetry and control during the final landing burn.

The upper stage reached space and completed a full insertion burn. The goal was to deploy a test payload (Starlink simulators) for the first time. Unfortunately, a stuck payload door prevented deployment. Additionally, a fuel leak was detected, causing a loss of attitude control, which led to the ship's destruction during atmospheric reentry.

---

## Innovations and Lessons Learned

- **Super Heavy Reuse:** Validated the recovery and successive flight concept for the largest stage ever built.
- **Payload Mechanics:** The payload door failure led to a complete redesign of the opening mechanism for subsequent flights.
- **Descent Efficiency:** A more aggressive angle of attack was tested during booster descent to save propellant.
