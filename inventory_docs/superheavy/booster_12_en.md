# Super Heavy Booster 12 (B12)

**Booster 12 (B12)** is a prototype of the first stage of SpaceX's Starship system, belonging to the **Block 2** architecture. It is historically significant as the first orbital-class rocket booster in history to be caught mid-air by launch tower arms after returning to Earth.

## Technical Specifications

*   **Height:** 71 meters (233 ft).
*   **Diameter:** 9 meters (30 ft).
*   **Propulsion:** 33 Raptor 2 engines (fuel: Subcooled liquid methane and liquid oxygen).
*   **Total Thrust:** Approximately 73.5 MN (16.5 million lbf).
*   **Material:** 300-series stainless steel.
*   **Architecture:** Block 2 (inclusive of shielding and electronics upgrades).

## Mission History (IFT-5)

On October 13, 2024, during the fifth integrated flight test (**IFT-5**), Booster 12 launched from Starbase. After stage separation, it performed a flawless boostback maneuver toward the launch site.

Instead of landing on conventional legs, the booster targeted the **Mechazilla** launch tower. Using its reinforced load points, the **Chopstick** arms closed around the vehicle while it was hovering, achieving a successful catch on the very first attempt.

## Innovations and Legacy

*   **Load Points:** Specific structural reinforcements for the catch sequence.
*   **Legless Design:** Massive weight savings by delegating the "landing" to ground infrastructure.
*   **Rapid Reusability:** Demonstrated the feasibility of returning the booster directly to the launch mount for subsequent flights.

Following the success of the mission, Booster 12 was retired from active service and is currently on display at the Starbase **Rocket Garden** as a monument to this milestone in aerospace engineering.
