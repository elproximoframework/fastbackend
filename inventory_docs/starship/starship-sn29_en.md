# Starship SN29 — Surviving the Reentry Inferno

**Starship SN29** (Ship 29) marked one of the most exciting and visually stunning milestones in SpaceX history during the fourth integrated flight test (IFT-4). It was the first ship to survive a full reentry from space and perform a controlled soft splashdown in the Indian Ocean.

## The Goal: Soft Splashdown

Launched on June 6, 2024, SN29's primary objective was not just to reach space but to demonstrate that a 50-meter upper stage could traverse the atmosphere and land in a controlled manner. To achieve this, SpaceX implemented significant improvements in flap robustness and thermal shield sealing.

## Technical Features

SN29 was designed with reinforced thermal armor and an improved attitude control system to handle the extreme turbulence of reentry.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Height | 50 m |
| Diameter | 9 m |
| Propulsion | 6 Raptor 2 Engines |
| Mission | IFT-4 (June 6, 2024) |
| Milestone | First soft splashdown in the Indian Ocean |

## The Epic Reentry of June 6

Through Starlink cameras, the world watched as SN29 was enveloped in purple plasma at hypersonic speeds. Despite suffering severe damage to one of its forward flaps due to extreme heat, the ship:
1. Maintained attitude control throughout the entire descent.
2. Traversed the densest layers of the atmosphere.
3. Executed the "flip" maneuver and ignited its engines to brake.
4. Performed a vertical soft splashdown at the target point in the Indian Ocean.

## Historical Significance

SN29 proved that the stainless steel construction philosophy is capable of withstanding orbital reentry even with structural damage. Its success validated SpaceX's aerobraking models and opened the door to true upper-stage reusability.

## Final Status

After completing its soft landing in the water, SN29 sank in a controlled manner in the Indian Ocean. Data from this flight allowed SpaceX to design an entirely new thermal shield for subsequent versions of the vehicle.
