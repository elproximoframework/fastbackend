# Starship SN28 — Reaching Orbital Velocity

**Starship SN28** (Ship 28) was the centerpiece of the third integrated flight test (IFT-3), marking a turning point in the Starship program's development. It was the first vehicle to reach orbital velocity and to demonstrate complex operational capabilities in space, such as the cargo door opening and propellant transfer.

## IFT-3 Milestones

Launched on March 14, 2024, from Starbase, SN28 was powered by Booster 10. During the mission, the vehicle successfully cleared all critical ascent phases, achieving for the first time a near-orbital trajectory that took it across the planet toward the Indian Ocean.

## Technical Features

SN28 included improvements in Raptor engine reliability and an optimized version of the thermal protection system (TPS).

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Height | 50 m |
| Diameter | 9 m |
| Propulsion | 6 Raptor 2 Engines |
| Mission | IFT-3 (March 14, 2024) |
| Innovation | Functional payload door and LOX transfer |

## Space Demonstrations

During its space coast phase, SN28 performed two fundamental tests for the program's future:
1. **Payload door opening and closing:** Validated the mechanism that will allow for the deployment of large-scale Starlink satellites.
2. **Propellant transfer:** Internal liquid oxygen transfer between tanks was performed—a key test for NASA's Artemis program.

## Historical Significance

SN28 proved that Starship is a functional orbital vehicle. It was the first time a ship of this scale operated in the space environment, validating not only the launch but also the commercial and logistical operations that define the ship's purpose.

## Final Status

After an extremely aggressive atmospheric reentry over the Indian Ocean, contact with SN28 was lost at an altitude of approximately 65 km. Although the vehicle did not survive the final impact, the data collected regarding plasma and thermal protection was invaluable for subsequent missions.
