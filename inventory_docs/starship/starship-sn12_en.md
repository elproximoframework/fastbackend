# Starship SN12, SN13, and SN14 — The Versions That Never Flew

In SpaceX's fast-paced development cycle, the motto is "build, test, learn." This iterative process sometimes moves so quickly that certain models become obsolete before they are even finished. This was the fate of the **SN12**, **SN13**, and **SN14** prototypes.

## The Design Left Behind

| Prototype | Final Status | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| SN12 | Scrapped (Jan 2021) | Design superseded by SN15 improvements |
| SN13 | Cancelled | Priority shifted to new avionics architecture |
| SN14 | Cancelled | Direct technological leap to SN15 |

As the flights of SN8 through SN11 provided critical data on structural integrity and Raptor engine behavior, SpaceX designed a comprehensive series of "hundreds of design improvements" to the ship's structure, avionics, and plumbing.

## Why Were They Skipped?

SpaceX decided it was not worth the time and resources to finish and fly ships with an "old" design when the new architecture (represented by SN15) was already ready for assembly.

- **Resource Optimization:** Instead of conducting more tests with old hardware that had already provided as much information as possible, the team focused on the next major iteration.
- **Lessons from SN11:** The loss of SN11 confirmed that deep changes were needed in avionics protection and thrust puck design, both of which were already integrated into SN15.

This strategic move demonstrated SpaceX's extreme agility: if a piece on the assembly line is no longer the best path to the final goal (orbit and Mars), it is discarded without hesitation to make way for the best available version.
