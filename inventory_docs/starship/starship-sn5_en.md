# Starship SN5 — The First Giant Leap

**Starship SN5** is remembered as one of the most iconic milestones in SpaceX's history. It was the first full-scale prototype of the Starship system to perform a controlled low-altitude flight (a "150-meter hop"), validating the flight control systems, leg landing deployment, and Raptor engine performance under real flight conditions.

## Configuration and Unique Features

Unlike later prototypes, SN5 had an unusual appearance: a stainless steel cylinder without a nose cone or aerodynamic surfaces. Instead, it carried a mass simulator at the top to mimic the center of gravity of a completed ship.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Height | ~30 m (Full tank section) |
| Diameter | 9 m |
| Propulsion | 1 Raptor (SN27) |
| Payload | Top-mounted mass simulator |
| Milestone | 150m Hop (August 4, 2020) |

The Raptor engine (SN27) was mounted asymmetrically (off-center) to accommodate future three-engine configurations. This required the vehicle to lift off with a slight angle (a "powerslide") to center the thrust beneath its center of mass.

## The Legendary 150-Meter Flight

On August 4, 2020, SN5 successfully lifted off from the launch pad in Boca Chica, Texas. During the flight, which lasted approximately 45 seconds, the vehicle:
1.  Ascended to an altitude of **150 meters**.
2.  Performed lateral translation maneuvers to reposition itself.
3.  Deployed its six internal landing legs.
4.  Executed a soft and precise landing on the adjacent pad.

## Historical Significance

The success of SN5 was the first visual demonstration of the next-generation "reusable spacecraft" concept. It was definitive proof that SpaceX could control a 9-meter diameter vehicle (a "flying silo") with a single Raptor engine.

## Final Status

Following the successful hop, SN5 was retired to make way for more advanced testing with **SN6**. Its legacy lives on in every current Starship, having been the pioneer that proved Elon Musk's stainless steel design could successfully take off and land.
