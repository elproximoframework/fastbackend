# Starship SN36 — A Setback on the Road to IFT-10

**Starship SN36** (Ship 36) was a Block 2 series prototype originally intended to star in the tenth integrated flight test (IFT-10). However, its history ended prematurely in mid-June 2025 due to a catastrophic failure during ground preparation tests.

## Incident at Massey’s Test Site

During a propellant loading session for a static fire test at the Massey’s test site, SN36 suffered a structural anomaly that led to an explosion. Although the incident resulted in the total loss of the vehicle, it provided critical data on the pressure limits of Block 2 tanks under rapid loading conditions.

## Planned Specifications

SN36 was set to be the first vehicle to attempt an even more aggressive approach maneuver toward the launch tower, using a refined version of the guidance software.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Height | 51 m |
| Diameter | 9 m |
| Propulsion | 6 Raptor 3 Engines |
| Objective | IFT-10 (Originally) |
| Final Status | Lost in ground test (June 2025) |

## Impact on the Program

The loss of SN36 forced SpaceX to accelerate the preparation of Ship 37 to avoid delaying the 2025 flight schedule. The analysis of the debris confirmed that a minor defect in a peripheral weld, combined with the new operating pressures of the Raptor 3 engines, was the root cause. This finding led to an immediate review of X-ray processes at the Starfactory.

## Historical Significance

Although it never flew, SN36 is remembered as the vehicle that allowed "clearing" the final manufacturing errors before the Starship program reached an unprecedented streak of success with subsequent prototypes. Its sacrifice ensured that later ships were structurally infallible.
