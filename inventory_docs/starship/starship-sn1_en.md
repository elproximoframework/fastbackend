# Starship SN1 — The First SN-Series Prototype

**Starship SN1** was the first vehicle in SpaceX's "SN" (Serial Number) Starship series. Built in Boca Chica (Starbase), Texas, it marked a fundamental shift in SpaceX's construction methodology: moving from horizontal stainless steel fabrication to building rings vertically in place, dramatically accelerating the assembly process.

## History and Context

SN1 was the direct successor to **Starship Mk1**, which had been destroyed during a cryogenic test in November 2019. SpaceX took the lesson to heart, abandoning the Mk1/Mk2 design in favor of a faster, more iterative build approach.

The philosophy behind SN1 was simple but revolutionary: **build fast, test fast, learn fast**. It didn't matter if the vehicle failed — what mattered was identifying design weaknesses as quickly as possible.

## Technical Specifications

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Height | ~50 m (aft section / tank only) |
| Diameter | 9 m |
| Material | 301X stainless steel (SpaceX alloy) |
| Planned engines | 3× Raptor SL |
| Final status | Destroyed |

## The Cryogenic Proof Test — February 28, 2020

On February 28, 2020, SN1 underwent a **cryogenic proof test**. The goal was to pressurize the propellant tanks with liquid nitrogen at extremely low temperatures to verify the vehicle's structural integrity.

The test ended in failure. The **"thrust puck"** — the structural piece at the base connecting the propellant tank to the Raptor engines — buckled under pressure. The failure was catastrophic: SN1 disintegrated within seconds, launching the forward section of the vehicle into the air.

## Lessons Learned

SN1's collapse was not a programmatic failure. On the contrary, it provided critical data on the thrust puck design that was immediately incorporated into **SN2** and all subsequent prototypes. SN2 passed the equivalent cryogenic test successfully just weeks later.

This "build → test → fail → learn → improve" cycle would become the defining feature of the Starship program throughout 2020–2021.

## Legacy

SN1 marks the starting point of what would become one of the most ambitious and fast-paced rocket test programs in the history of astronautics. From SN1 to SN15 (first successful high-altitude flight, May 2021), SpaceX built and tested more than a dozen prototypes in under 18 months.
