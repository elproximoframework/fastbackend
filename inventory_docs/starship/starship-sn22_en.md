# Ship 22 — The Destiny of the "Rocket Garden"

**Ship 22** (S22) was a Starship prototype that completed its structural assembly in early 2022. Although it was a high-fidelity craft with its heat shield and aerodynamics ready, it never saw the fire of its engines in a real test, ending its life as a display piece before being recycled.

## Prototype Summary

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Height | ~50 m |
| Propulsion | 6 Raptor Engines (3 SL, 3 RVac) |
| Status | Scrapped (Mid-2022) |
| Reason | Skipped in favor of the massive improvements on Ship 24 |

Ship 22 was moved to the Starbase storage area known as the **"Rocket Garden"** after leaving the High Bay. There, it stood alongside Ship 16 and other historic prototypes, serving as a visual reminder of SpaceX's rapid pace of development.

## Why Was It Retired?

Similar to S21, Ship 22 was overtaken by the program's technical evolution:
1.  **Avionics and Wiring:** Electronic systems and internal wiring evolved drastically during 2022. Upgrading Ship 22 to the new standards for communication with mission control and the Booster was more costly than building a new ship from scratch.
2.  **Heat Shield Optimization:** SpaceX changed the fastening method for some thermal tiles to reduce the risk of shedding during flight. Ship 22 used the older system, making it less suitable for a high-risk orbital test.
3.  **Focus on IFT-1:** Any hardware that was not "perfect" for the first Integrated Flight Test (IFT-1) was discarded to avoid delaying the schedule. The team decided to focus exclusively on **Ship 24** and **Booster 7**.

## The Legacy of S22

Ship 22 was finally scrapped in mid-2022. Although its life was short and static, its construction allowed SpaceX to perfect the assembly speed at the "Starfactory," reaching production rates that would soon allow for the manufacture of a complete Starship every few weeks.
