# Starship SN26 — Propellant Transfer Test Article

**Starship SN26** (Ship 26) was an upper-stage Starship prototype that, unlike its immediate predecessors, was designed without aerodynamic surfaces (flaps) or a full thermal protection system. Its primary purpose was to serve as a ground test platform for critical life support systems and, more importantly, for research into in-space propellant transfer.

## Context and Function

Built during the height of the V1 series, SN26 represented a specialized variant. Without flaps, it was clear from its inception that it was not intended for a controlled atmospheric reentry. Instead, it was used to validate the internal tank design and plumbing systems required for future orbital refueling missions.

## Features of SN26

SN26's simplified design allowed SpaceX to focus on internal architecture and the reliability of header tanks.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Height | 50 m |
| Diameter | 9 m |
| Propulsion | 6 Raptor Engines (Test configuration) |
| Purpose | Propellant transfer testing and internal systems validation |
| Result | Retired after ground testing campaign |

## Testing Campaign

Throughout 2023, SN26 underwent multiple cryogenic loading tests and pressure simulations at Starbase. These tests were fundamental to understanding how cryogenic fluids behave under different pressure conditions—data essential for the propellant transfer demonstration eventually performed by Ship 28 in space.

## Historical Importance

Although SN26 did not fly, its role was vital in de-risking one of the Starship program's most complex technologies: transferring thousands of tons of fuel between ships in orbit, a prerequisite for NASA's Artemis program and missions to Mars.

## Final Status

After completing its ground test program and being surpassed by advancements in Ship 28 and subsequent vehicles, SN26 was retired and scrapped at Starbase in late 2023.
