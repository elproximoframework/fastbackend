# Starship SN11 — Mystery in the Fog

**Starship SN11** followed in the footsteps of SN10, but it proved just how unforgiving the Boca Chica test environment can be. Launched on March 30, 2021, under fog so dense it obscured the vehicle even from ground observers, SN11 performed a near-flawless flight until its final moments, when a catastrophic internal systems failure caused it to disintegrate mid-air before touchdown.

## Flight Profile and Conditions

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Altitude | 10 km |
| Diameter | 9 m |
| Propulsion | 3 Raptor Engines |
| Conditions | Dense fog (zero visibility) |
| Outcome | Mid-air RUD during landing burn |

The ascent was nominal, with the engines shutting down sequentially as planned. The craft successfully performed the "belly-flop" maneuver, navigating through the thick fog using only its internal sensors and GPS.

## The Relight Anomaly

Approximately 5 minutes and 49 seconds into the flight, as SN11 initiated its relight sequence for the final landing flip, the vehicle exploded spectacularly. Due to the fog, only flashes of light and falling debris could be seen from the ground.

### Technical Cause of Failure
Following a post-flight investigation, Elon Musk revealed the cause:
1.  **Methane Leak:** A small methane leak in Engine 2 triggered a fire.
2.  **Avionics Damage:** The fire "fried" parts of the control systems (avionics).
3.  **Hard Start:** This led to a "hard start" in the methane turbopump during the relight attempt, causing the immediate destruction of the engine and, subsequently, the entire ship.

## Legacy and Improvements

SN11 was the last of the "old design" prototype series. Its loss accelerated the implementation of critical safety improvements in plumbing and electronic system insulation—changes that would be reflected in the radically improved design of **SN15**.
