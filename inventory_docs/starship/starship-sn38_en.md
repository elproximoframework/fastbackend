# Starship SN38 — Closing the Block 2 Era

**Starship SN38** (Ship 38) was the last Block 2 series prototype to participate in a high-complexity integrated flight (IFT-11) on October 13, 2025. Its mission represented the culmination of all lessons learned since SN33, sealing the reliability of the V2 architecture before the jump to the V3 version.

## A Reentry Veteran

During IFT-11, SN38 demonstrated unprecedented thermal resistance. It was the first ship to test a new experimental ablative coating on the flap hinges to further reduce maintenance between flights, achieving 100% structural survival.

## The Final Block 2 Flight

Launched with Booster 17, SN38 performed:
1. A nominal ascent phase with perfect "hot-staging" stage separation.
2. A simulated internal fuel transfer demonstration to prepare for future NASA HLS (Human Landing System) missions.
3. A night descent maneuver in the Indian Ocean, proving the navigation sensors' capability in absolute darkness.

## Historical Significance

SN38 closed SpaceX's most successful development stage to date. With its flight, the company confirmed that the Block 2 design was capable of meeting basic requirements for the Artemis program, allowing test hardware to transition into mass-production hardware.

## Final Status

Following a soft vertical splashdown, SN38 sank in the Indian Ocean on October 13, 2025. Its success was the definitive signal for SpaceX to retire the V2 series molds and focus exclusively on the revolutionary Starship V3 architecture.
