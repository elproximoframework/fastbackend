# Ship 20 (SN20) — The First Orbital Starship

**Ship 20** (formerly known as SN20) marked the beginning of a new era in the Starship program. It was the first ship designed from the ground up to survive the extreme temperatures of atmospheric reentry and reach orbital velocity, serving as the partner for **Booster 4** in the first full orbital flight attempt.

## Orbital Architecture

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Height | 50 m |
| Propulsion | 6 Raptor Engines (3 SL, 3 RVac) |
| Heat Shield | ~18,000 hexagonal ceramic tiles |
| Milestone | First ship with full heat shield and 6 engines |
| Outcome | Retired after exhaustive ground testing |

### Principal Innovations
- **6-Engine Configuration:** Ship 20 was the first to mount three sea-level Raptor engines and three **Raptor Vacuum (RVac)** engines, featuring massive nozzles for maximum efficiency in the vacuum of space.
- **Full Heat Shield:** For the first time, nearly half of the fuselage was covered by thousands of small ceramic tiles mechanically attached. These pieces are vital for protecting the stainless steel from the heat of reentry.
- **Redesigned Flaps:** The flap design was refined to improve control during both atmospheric flight and the return from space.

## Test Campaign

Ship 20 was the star of an intense test campaign at the Starbase orbital pad:
1.  **Static Fires:** On November 12, 2021, Ship 20 made history by igniting its **6 Raptor engines** simultaneously for the first time on the ground—an unprecedented demonstration of power for a prototype.
2.  **Booster Integration:** Multiple "stacking" tests were performed on Booster 4, forming the tallest launch vehicle ever built (120 meters).

## Legacy and Retirement

Although Ship 20 never performed its orbital flight due to FAA licensing delays and SpaceX's shift toward the improved "V1.1" version (Ship 24), its importance is immeasurable. It validated the heat shield mounting system and demonstrated that the 6-engine configuration was viable.

Today, Ship 20 is remembered as the prototype that transformed Starship from a "suborbital hopper" into a true space vehicle capable of conquering Earth's orbit.
