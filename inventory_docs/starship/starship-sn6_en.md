# Starship SN6 — Repeating the Feat

**Starship SN6** was the second full-scale prototype to perform a 150-meter test flight. Launched just a month after the success of SN5, SN6 consolidated SpaceX's operational capability to conduct iterative launches and precise landings, demonstrating the robustness of the ship's basic design.

## Milestones and Configuration

Like its predecessor, SN6 lacked a nose cone and aerodynamic surfaces, using a mass simulator on its upper section to balance the center of gravity.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Height | ~30 m (Full tank section) |
| Diameter | 9 m |
| Propulsion | 1 Raptor (SN29) |
| Milestone | 150m Hop (September 3, 2020) |
| Outcome | Total success and soft landing |

The vehicle used the **SN29** Raptor engine, performing a successful static fire on August 23 before its final flight.

## The Second 150-Meter Hop

On September 3, 2020, SN6 lifted off from Boca Chica. The flight was a near-exact repetition of the SN5 mission profile:
- Controlled liftoff with a single Raptor engine.
- Ascent to 150 meters with a slight lateral lean to center the thrust.
- Leg deployment and soft landing on the target pad.

## Legacy and Final Status

The success of SN6 allowed SpaceX to retire this low-altitude "hop" testing phase and focus on much more complex prototypes. SN6 proved that SN5's success was not a fluke but the result of a highly reliable flight control system. Following its flight, SN6 was retired and dismantled in January 2021 to make way for the era of high-altitude flights that would begin with SN8.
