# Ship 21 — The Transition Prototype

**Ship 21** (S21) was a Starship prototype that served as a bridge between first-generation designs (like Ship 20) and the optimized versions intended for the first orbital flight. Although its sections were completed, the ship was ultimately scrapped before it could be stacked.

## Specifications and Development

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Diameter | 9 m |
| Propulsion | Designed for 6 Raptor Engines |
| Status | Scrapped (April 2022) |
| Reason | Obsolescence in the face of Ship 24's design |

Assembly of Ship 21's tank and nose sections began in late 2021. For months, progress was observed at the Starbase facilities, with expectations that it would be the next in line for testing after Ship 20.

## Why Was It Discarded?

The cancellation of Ship 21 was due to a fundamental change in the vehicle's interfaces:
1.  **Booster Compatibility:** SpaceX made significant changes to the Quick Disconnect (QD) system and the attachment points with the Super Heavy Booster. Ship 21 was not compatible with the updates introduced for **Booster 7**.
2.  **Design Iteration:** The design for Ship 24 was already well underway, offering structural and weight improvements that made Ship 21 less efficient for a real flight test.
3.  **Manufacturing Lessons:** It served as an excellent manufacturing exercise for the Starbase team, refining welding processes and thermal protection tile placement on the new production line.

## The End of S21

In April 2022, the sections of Ship 21 were moved to the scrap yard. Parts of the vehicle were cut and recycled, symbolizing SpaceX's philosophy of not clinging to old hardware if a better and faster path to success exists.
