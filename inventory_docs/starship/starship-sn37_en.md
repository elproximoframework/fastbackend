# Starship SN37 — Total Success on IFT-10

**Starship SN37** (Ship 37) marked a historic milestone on August 26, 2025, by successfully completing the **IFT-10** mission. Following the loss of Ship 36, SN37 took on the responsibility of proving that the Block 2 architecture was not only robust but also capable of complex orbital operations.

## A Flight of Multiple Achievements

Launched from Starbase alongside Booster 16, SN37 met all its primary and secondary objectives. It was the first ship to deploy Starlink satellite simulators using the "Pez dispenser" mechanism under real vacuum conditions, validating the design for future commercial missions.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Height | 51 m |
| Diameter | 9 m |
| Propulsion | 6 Raptor 3 Engines |
| Mission | IFT-10 (August 26, 2025) |
| Milestone | Starlink simulator deployment and orbital relight |

## Technological Demonstrations

During IFT-10, SN37 performed:
1. A successful deployment of 8 payload simulators.
2. A second in-space burn of its Raptor engines to adjust the reentry trajectory.
3. A perfect atmospheric reentry with minimal thermal shield wear.
4. A controlled vertical splashdown in the Indian Ocean with unprecedented precision.

## Historical Significance

The success of SN37 consolidated Starship as a functional space transport system. With data from this flight, SpaceX obtained the internal certification necessary to begin planning the deployment of actual Starlink V3 satellites and the first ship-to-ship fuel transfer tests.

## Final Status

After meeting all mission parameters, SN37 performed its final splashdown in the Indian Ocean on August 26, 2025. The vehicle sank after completing the tank purge sequence, leaving a legacy of total reliability for the Block 2 series.
