# Starship SN32 — The Liaison Prototype

**Starship SN32** (Ship 32) was a transition prototype that did not perform an integrated flight, but whose construction was fundamental for implementing the assembly lines for the new **Block 2** generation. Its role was primarily industrial and focused on manufacturing process validation.

## Context and Function

Assembled in late 2024, SN32 was originally conceived as an advanced V1 ship. However, with the accelerated success of IFT-5 and IFT-6, SpaceX decided to make the technological leap to the Block 2 version sooner than expected. SN32 was used to test the integration of new internal components that would later become standard on Ship 33.

## Features of SN32

Although outwardly similar to Ship 31, internally SN32 featured a new wiring layout and redundant avionics systems.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Height | 50 m |
| Diameter | 9 m |
| Propulsion | 6 Raptor 2 Engines (Test configuration) |
| Purpose | Industrial process validation and transition to Block 2 |
| Result | Retired without flight |

## Ground Testing

During its stay in Starbase's "High Bay," SN32 served as a model for training assembly teams on the new welding techniques required for the larger tanks of the V2 version. It was also used for fit-testing the new electric actuators for the flaps.

## Historical Importance

SN32 represents the turning point where SpaceX prioritized hardware evolution over flight numbers. By retiring a functional ship to accelerate a superior version, the company once again demonstrated its commitment to rapid iteration and constant improvement.

## Final Status

In early 2025, SN32 was retired from the active flight line and dismantled. Its components were recycled or used as spare parts for other ground test articles, yielding all center stage to Ship 33—the first of the new Block 2 series.
