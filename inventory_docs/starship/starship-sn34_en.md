# Starship SN34 — Consolidating the Block 2 Version

**Starship SN34** (Ship 34) was the second Block 2 series prototype to perform an integrated flight. Launched during the IFT-8 mission on February 24, 2025, its goal was to validate the repeatability and reliability of the new V2 architecture under extreme flight conditions.

## Refining Block 2

Building on the resounding success of IFT-7, SN34 introduced additional improvements in the rocket's base aerodynamics to enhance airflow during splashdown. It was a key ship for testing the new header tanks integrated more efficiently into the nose.

## Technical Features

SN34 was notable for its extensive use of the new Raptor 3 engines, which eliminate much of the external wiring and plumbing, reducing the risk of mechanical fires.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Height | 51 m |
| Diameter | 9 m |
| Propulsion | 6 Raptor 3 Engines |
| Mission | IFT-8 (February 24, 2025) |
| Innovation | Advanced Raptor 3 integration and base aerodynamics |

## A Flight Toward Recovery

During IFT-8, SN34 didn't just repeat Ship 33's milestones; it exceeded them in precision:
1. It achieved perfect engine burn times throughout all phases.
2. It kept its thermal shield intact after surpassing the reentry heat peak.
3. It simulated a catch maneuver at sea with centimeter-level precision, proving that the guidance software was ready to attempt tower landings.

## Historical Significance

SN34 proved that the Block 2 design was not a "one-off success" but a robust and repeatable platform. Its data were fundamental to SpaceX's decision that the next ship, Ship 35, could attempt even riskier maneuvers.

## Final Status

SN34 performed a successful vertical splashdown in the Indian Ocean on February 24, 2025. After completing its data collection mission, the vehicle was sunk in a controlled manner, leaving the path clear for the challenges of IFT-9.
