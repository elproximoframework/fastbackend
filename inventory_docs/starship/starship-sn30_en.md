# Starship SN30 — The Era of Precision

**Starship SN30** (Ship 30) was the upper-stage vehicle that participated in the historic fifth integrated flight test (IFT-5) on October 13, 2024. While the mission is primarily remembered for the Super Heavy booster catch, Ship 30 demonstrated unprecedented precision and resilience during its flight and subsequent splashdown.

## IFT-5 Milestones

Launched alongside Booster 12, SN30 benefited from a total redesign of the thermal protection system. Following the successful hot-staging separation and the historic capture of the booster by the tower's arms (Mechazilla), Ship 30 continued its mission to test the limits of reentry precision.

## Technical Features

SN30 introduced a new adhesive for the thermal tiles and secondary shielding on the flap hinges to prevent the damage observed in previous missions.

## A Flawless Reentry

Unlike Ship 29, Ship 30 traversed the atmosphere without major flap burn-through incidents. Attitude control was extremely stable, allowing the ship to:
1. Navigate through plasma with minimal thermal shield degradation.
2. Execute a millimeter-precision approach to the landing target.
3. Perform the landing burn and splash down softly in the Indian Ocean, transmitting high-quality data until the final second.

## Historical Significance

SN30 confirmed that Starship is ready for longer and more complex missions. It validated the new thermal shield design, allowing SpaceX to focus on the next major phase: the recovery and reuse of the upper stage on dry land.

## Final Status

After a perfect splashdown, SN30 was sunk in a controlled manner in the Indian Ocean. Its success marked the end of the V1 generation test series, paving the way for the entry into service of the V2 (Block 2) generation with increased propellant capacity and aerodynamic improvements.
