# Starship SN39 — The V3 Version Revolution

**Starship SN39** (Ship 39) represents the most significant technological leap since the program's inception, being the first prototype of the **Starship V3 (Block 3) generation**. Its debut is scheduled for the **IFT-12** mission in April 2026, ushering in a new era of power and efficiency.

## Starship V3 Innovations

SN39 introduces radical changes: it is significantly taller than its predecessors, with a refined stainless-steel structure that drastically reduces dry mass. Its Raptor 3 engines are natively integrated into a redesigned engine bay allowing for much higher thrust and superior cooling.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Height | 53 m (New extended tanks) |
| Diameter | 9 m |
| Propulsion | 6 Raptor 3 Engines (V3 configuration) |
| Mission | IFT-12 (April 2026 - Planned) |
| Milestone | Starship V3 and Pad 2 Architecture Debut |

## SN39 Objectives

For its maiden flight, SN39's mission is to:
1. Validate the structural integrity of the new extended tanks under maximum loads.
2. Demonstrate the performance improvement of the Raptor 3 propulsion system in orbit.
3. Attempt a high-precision approach to prepare for the first upper-stage catch attempt.
4. Be the first ship launched from the new Pad 2 tower at Starbase.

## Historical Significance

SN39 is not just a prototype; it is the base model for mass cargo transport to Mars. Its design, optimized for rapid production at the Starfactory, allows SpaceX to aim for weekly launch cadences. With Ship 39, the concept of a fully reusable rocket reaches its most advanced technological expression.

## Current Status

As of early April 2026, SN39 is at the Pad 2 launch site conducting final static fire tests. Its launch will mark the beginning of the massive operational deployment phase for the Starship system.
