# Starship SN40 — Mass Production at Starfactory

**Starship SN40** (Ship 40) is the second prototype of the **V3 (Block 3) generation** and represents the state of the art in SpaceX's aerospace manufacturing. Unlike the initial ships, SN40 is being manufactured using massive automation processes at the Starfactory in Starbase, Texas.

## V3 Design Maturity

As part of the V3 series, SN40 inherits all the structural improvements introduced on Ship 39: extended propellant tanks, a significant reduction in the number of parts, and the exclusive use of high-efficiency Raptor 3 engines.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Height | 53 m |
| Diameter | 9 m |
| Propulsion | 6 Raptor 3 Engines |
| Generation | V3 (Block 3) |
| Current Status | In production / Final assembly (April 2026) |

## Manufacturing Innovations

SN40 is an exponent of Elon Musk's vision of "the machine that builds the machine." Its construction stands out for:
1. Use of new stainless-steel alloys that are lighter and more heat-resistant.
2. A 20% reduction in assembly time thanks to new automated production lines.
3. Optimization of the thermal protection system (TPS) applied robotically to ensure perfect gapless coverage.

## The Future of SN40

SN40 is expected to be the vehicle that allows SpaceX to reach an unprecedented launch cadence once the V3 system is validated with the flight of SN39. Its primary mission will be the final consolidation of the reusable fleet for low-orbit missions and the start of long-duration lunar missions.

## Current Status

As of April 1, 2026, SN40 is in the final stages of integration at Starfactory. It is expected to be moved to the Massey’s test site for its first cryogenic tests later this month, closely following the progress of its predecessor, Ship 39.
