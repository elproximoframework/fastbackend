# Starship SN27 — The Last of the Ground Test Articles

**Starship SN27** (Ship 27) was an upper-stage Starship prototype that concluded the phase of specialized ground test articles for the V1 generation. Similar to SN26, it was built without flaps or thermal protection, optimized for structural and internal systems testing.

## Context and Development

SN27 was assembled in 2023 as a sibling to SN26, but it incorporated subtle improvements in steel alloy and robotic welding techniques. Its primary function was to act as a "data backup" for propellant transfer tests and to validate the structure's resilience under extreme thermal stress cycles.

## Features of SN27

The vehicle focused on manufacturing efficiency and the integration of the new avionics systems that would be used in integrated flights.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Height | 50 m |
| Diameter | 9 m |
| Propulsion | 6 Raptor Engines (Mass simulators/Test) |
| Purpose | Structural validation and test data redundancy |
| Result | Retired after pressure testing |

## Key Testing

During its stay at the Starbase test area, SN27 underwent rigorous "cryo-proofing" (cryogenic testing) where it was filled with liquid nitrogen to simulate actual fuel loads. These tests helped refine the safety margins for the stainless steel tanks prior to the launch of Ship 28.

## Historical Importance

SN27 marked the end of an era in which SpaceX built entire ships solely for exhaustive ground testing. From this point on, confidence in simulation models and flight data allowed nearly all subsequent prototypes to be destined directly for launch campaigns.

## Final Status

SN27 was retired from the active test campaign in mid-2023. Its lessons allowed for the accelerated preparation of Ship 28 for IFT-3, proving that the phase of rapid ground prototyping had achieved its goal.
