# Starship SN35 — The End of the Oceanic Testing Phase

**Starship SN35** (Ship 35) was the leading vehicle for the ninth integrated flight test (IFT-9), conducted on May 27, 2025. This ship represented the full maturity of the Block 2 platform, achieving control and thermal resistance that allowed SpaceX to plan the next major step: landing the upper stage on the launch tower.

## Technological Consolidation

SN35 incorporated a "second generation" thermal shield for Block 2, featuring more resilient tiles and a much more advanced real-time monitoring system. It was the first vehicle to fly with a Raptor 3 engine configuration fully optimized for series production.

## The IFT-9 Mission

Launched alongside Booster 15, Ship 35 executed a flawless mission:
1. It performed a perfect ascent phase, reaching the intended trajectory with zero deviations.
2. It traversed the atmosphere in a reentry that, for the first time, left no significant visible damage on the flap hinges or burns on the structure.
3. It executed a landing maneuver so precise that the vehicle "sat" on a virtual area the size of the launch tower in the Indian Ocean.

## Historical Significance

SN35's success marked the end of the need for ocean splashdowns to collect reentry data. The ship proved that the Block 2 design was safe and predictable enough to attempt catching the upper stage with the "Mechazilla" arms in subsequent missions.

## Final Status

After performing its vertical splashdown and sending the final packets of high-resolution telemetry data, SN35 was sunk in a controlled manner in the Indian Ocean on May 27, 2025. Its success was the definitive green light for Ship 36 to attempt the first return to the tower at Starbase.
