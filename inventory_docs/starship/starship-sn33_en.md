# Starship SN33 — The Dawn of Block 2

**Starship SN33** (Ship 33) is one of the most emblematic ships in SpaceX history, being the first **Block 2 (V2) generation** prototype to fly. Its launch on January 16, 2025, marked a massive technological leap, introducing a refined architecture following the successes of the V1 series.

## Block 2 Innovations

Ship 33 was visually distinct from its predecessors by its thinner forward flaps, repositioned leeward to protect them from heat. Furthermore, its structure included a taller tank section and elliptical domes, significantly increasing its propellant capacity.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Height | 51 m (1 meter extra over V1) |
| Diameter | 9 m |
| Propulsion | 6 Raptor 3 Engines |
| Mission | IFT-7 (January 16, 2025) |
| Innovation | Redesigned flaps and increased tank volume |

## The IFT-7 Milestone

During its maiden flight, SN33 demonstrated the superiority of the new design. With Raptor 3 engines—more powerful and reliable—the ship:
1. Reached space with greater energy efficiency due to its reduced structural weight.
2. Tested the new high-density thermal shielding in high-stress areas.
3. Proved that the flap repositioning allowed for much more precise and safe reentry control against extreme plasma temperatures.

## Historical Significance

SN33 was proof that SpaceX could scale Starship production toward its definitive configuration. Its success validated that the ship was not only capable of flying but of doing so with safety margins and efficiency ready for commercial operations and lunar missions.

## Final Status

Following a perfect reentry and impeccable stability control, SN33 performed a soft splashdown in the Indian Ocean. It was the first Block 2 vehicle to complete its mission profile in its entirety, paving the way for the mass production of the V2 series.
