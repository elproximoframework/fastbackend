# Starship SN15 — The Ultimate Success

**Starship SN15** represents one of the greatest milestones in modern aerospace history. Launched on May 5, 2021, it became the first full-scale Starship prototype to successfully complete a 10 km high-altitude flight and, most importantly, perform a perfect and safe landing without any post-landing explosions.

## The SN15 Revolution

Unlike its predecessors, SN15 incorporated hundreds of design improvements that made it a significantly more reliable spacecraft.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Height | ~50 m |
| Propulsion | 3 Improved Raptor Engines |
| Milestone | First fully successful landing (May 5, 2021) |
| Outcome | Total success; vehicle recovered intact |

### Key Improvements
- **Next-Generation Avionics:** A completely revamped and more resilient flight control system.
- **Production Raptors:** Utilized a more mature version of the Raptor engines with higher relight reliability.
- **Plumbing Redesign:** The fuel system was redesigned to prevent the leaks that led to SN11's demise.
- **Thermal Protection:** An increased number of thermal protection tiles to test reentry stress.

## The May 5, 2021 Flight

The flight was a masterclass in engineering demonstration:
1.  **Ascent:** Nominal liftoff and climb to 10 km, with flawless engine control.
2.  **Descent Maneuver:** The horizontal descent was stable and precise, utilizing thermal protection tiles in several areas.
3.  **Landing Flip & Touchdown:** While SN10 had landed using a single engine, SN15 optimized the use of two engines for the final touchdown, resulting in an extremely soft landing on the Boca Chica concrete pad.

## Historical Impact

The success of SN15 finally validated SpaceX's stainless steel architecture and the viability of the "belly-flop" landing maneuver. Following its landing, the ship stood static on the pad, with only small methane venting fires that were quickly extinguished by ground systems.

This success marked the end of the "suborbital" hop testing era for Starship, allowing SpaceX to focus its efforts on the first orbital launch with the **Starship & Super Heavy** stack.
