# Starship SN16 — The Resident of the "Rocket Garden"

**Starship SN16** was a high-altitude prototype nearly identical to the successful SN15. Although it was fully built and ready for flight, it never actually lifted off from the Boca Chica pad, instead becoming a display and study piece in SpaceX's famous "Rocket Garden."

## Specifications and Status

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Height | ~50 m |
| Propulsion | 3 Raptor Engines |
| Status | Never flew; Retired to the Rocket Garden |
| Reason | Total success of SN15 and shift to orbital priority |

SN16 represented the culmination of the first phase of suborbital testing. Following the flawless landing of SN15 on May 5, 2021, SpaceX determined that it already had sufficient data regarding 10 km flights.

## Why Didn't It Fly?

The decision to ground SN16 was a strategic one:
1.  **Data Saturation:** SN15 had already proven the design could launch, flip, and land without exploding. A flight by SN16 would have consumed time and resources without providing radically new information.
2.  **Focus on Orbital Flight:** SpaceX decided to accelerate the transition toward the first orbital flight. This required focusing all efforts on **Ship 20** (SN20) and the first Super Heavy Booster (**Booster 4**).
3.  **Engineering Milestone:** Although it didn't fly, SN16 served to validate the repeatability of the manufacturing process, demonstrating that SpaceX could systematically produce high-fidelity spacecraft.

## Final Fate

After spending months on display alongside other historic prototypes, SN16 was eventually scrapped to free up space at the Starbase facilities for newer generations of ships (V2) and orbital launch infrastructure. Its legacy is that of the ship that marked the end of Starship's "infancy."
