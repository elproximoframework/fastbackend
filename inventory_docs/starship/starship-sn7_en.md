# Starship SN7 and SN7.1 — The Tanks that Forged the Steel

The **SN7** and **SN7.1** prototypes were not flight vehicles but cryogenic pressure test articles dedicated to one fundamental goal: validating the transition from 301 stainless steel to **304L**, an alloy much more ductile at cryogenic temperatures.

## SN7: The First Step Towards 304L

Built in June 2020, SN7 was the first test tank manufactured entirely with 304L steel. Its objective was to test if the material could withstand liquid nitrogen pressure without suffering brittle failures (violent explosions).

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Material | 304L Stainless Steel |
| Purpose | Pressure test to failure (Cryogenic Proof) |
| Test 1 (Jun 15, 2020) | 7.6 bar (Success, controlled leak) |
| Test 2 (Jun 23, 2020) | Planned destruction (Welding validation) |

The success of SN7 demonstrated "leak-before-burst" capability, a vital safety property that allows leaks to be detected before a catastrophic explosion.

## SN7.1: Large-Scale Validation

SN7.1 was a much more robust tank that underwent an extensive testing campaign in September 2020.

- **September 23, 2020:** After several days of intense testing, SN7.1 was deliberately pushed to its bursting limit, exploding spectacularly at the Boca Chica launch site.
- **Outcome:** The test provided SpaceX engineers with the final structural resistance data needed for high-altitude missions.

## Importance in the Starship Program

Without the data from the SN7 and SN7.1 tanks, the flights of SN8 and later versions would not have been possible. These tanks validated that 304L steel was the optimal material to withstand the internal pressures and thermal load of the Starship program, becoming the manufacturing standard ever since.
