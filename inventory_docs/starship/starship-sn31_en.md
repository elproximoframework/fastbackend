# Starship SN31 — The Last of the V1 Generation

**Starship SN31** (Ship 31) closed a fundamental chapter in SpaceX history as the final Version 1 (V1) prototype to perform an integrated flight (IFT-6) on November 19, 2024. Its mission consolidated the reliability of the original design before the full transition to the Block 2 architecture.

## IFT-6 Milestones

Launched from Starbase, SN31 repeated the high-precision trajectory toward the Indian Ocean. However, its flight included an unprecedented technological objective: the first in-space ignition of one of its sea-level Raptor engines—a critical test for future deorbit and ground landing missions.

## Technical Features

As the culmination of the V1 series, SN31 incorporated all accumulated thermal protection improvements, including the new adhesive and reinforced flap joints.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|

## A Final Validation Flight

During IFT-6, SN31 demonstrated exceptional stability. After separating from Booster 13, the ship performed:
1. A successful relight of a Raptor engine in vacuum conditions.
2. A controlled atmospheric reentry with more aggressive angles of attack to test thermal limits.
3. A vertical soft splashdown in the Indian Ocean during daylight, providing the clearest images to date of the final landing process.

## Historical Significance

The success of SN31 allowed SpaceX to conclude the V1 version test phase. Using data from this flight, the engineering team validated that the control system was robust enough to handle the larger and heavier Block 2 ships.

## Final Status

After its successful splashdown, SN31 sank in the Indian Ocean on November 19, 2024. Its legacy is total confidence in the propulsion and guidance system now used by next-generation ships.
