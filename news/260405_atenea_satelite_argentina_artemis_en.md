# Atenea: The Argentine Pride Traveling Aboard the SLS Megarocket to the Moon

Aboard the **SLS** megarocket—the vehicle carrying the four **Artemis II** astronauts toward lunar orbit—travel four secondary payloads of high scientific value. One of them was built entirely in Argentina: the **Atenea** microsatellite.

## Southern Ingenuity in Deep Space

Atenea is a **12U CubeSat**, a device measuring just 30x20x20 centimeters and weighing about 15 kilograms. Named after the Greek goddess of wisdom and strategy, this satellite was developed through a joint effort between **CONAE**, national universities (UNLP, UNSAM, FIUBA), IAR, CNEA, and the company VENG SA.

Argentina holds the honor of being the only Latin American country invited by NASA to integrate a secondary payload in this historic mission, sharing space with developments from Germany, Saudi Arabia, and South Korea.

## Science Against Radiation

The primary objective of Atenea is to test various **radiation shielding** methods, one of the most critical obstacles to astronaut health in future lunar bases and journeys to Mars. Testing these materials in the actual deep space environment will allow for the design of safer spacecraft. So far, Argentine authorities confirm that the device is operating as planned autonomously after its release in space.

## The Role of CubeSats

Despite their small size, these satellites are powerful tools for research. By traveling as "ride-shares" on primary missions, they allow countries with emerging space programs to conduct cutting-edge experiments at a reduced cost, improving global understanding of the space environment outside Earth's protection.
