# PLD Space Successfully Validates Miura 5 "Strongback" in Teruel

Spanish company **PLD Space** has achieved a new milestone in the development of its orbital launcher, the **Miura 5**, after successfully completing tests of its vertical support system, known as the *strongback*. The tests were conducted at the company's facilities at Teruel Airport, using a full-scale mockup that accurately replicates the rocket's mass and inertia.

## Validation of Critical Systems

The *strongback* test allowed the engineering team to validate several essential components for launch success:
- **Hydraulic Systems:** Operation of the mast responsible for placing the rocket in a vertical position.
- **Mechanical Interfaces:** Coupling between the vehicle and its support structure.
- **Front Clamp:** Load resistance of the system that secures the rocket to the structure during pre-launch phases.

## Ground Infrastructure Optimization

This testing approach decouples vehicle integration from the launch pad, optimizing ground infrastructure and reducing response times. By overlapping operations, PLD Space improves operational efficiency to support a higher launch cadence in the future.

## Heading to French Guiana

Following the success in Teruel, the *strongback* structure is ready for shipment to the Guiana Space Centre (CSG) in Kourou, French Guiana. Part of the PLD Space team is already working on-site to prepare the launch complex for the upcoming orbital flight. In parallel, the company continues its financial expansion after recently raising 10 million euros in investment through Sego Venture.
