# Comet 41P/Tuttle–Giacobini–Kresak: An Unexpected Twist in Cometary Dynamics

A recent study published in *The Astronomical Journal* has revealed anomalous behavior in comet **41P/Tuttle–Giacobini–Kresak**. According to Hubble Space Telescope observations, this celestial body not only altered its rotation speed after its 2017 solar passage but likely reversed its direction of spin entirely.

## The Effect of Outgassing

The radical change in rotation is attributed to a well-known but rarely documented physical process of such intensity: **outgassing**. As it approaches the Sun, the comet's surface ice sublimates, releasing jets of gas. These jets act as irregular propulsion engines that generate forces capable of modifying the nucleus's torque.

In the case of 41P, these forces were so powerful that the rotation period measured eight months after perihelion was **0.60 days**, a figure significantly different from measurements taken prior to the solar encounter.

## A Small and Vulnerable Nucleus

The analysis conducted with Hubble's WFC3 camera determined that the comet's nucleus is especially small, with an **effective radius of just 500 meters**. Additionally, its shape is elongated, with an axis ratio of 1.4 to 1. These characteristics make it extremely sensitive to the forces generated by gas expulsion, allowing for rapid and drastic changes in its dynamic state.

## Scientific Implications

The discovery, led by astronomer David Jewitt, challenges conventional models of rotational stability in short-period comets. The data suggests that the physical life of the nucleus, under such constant stress, could be relatively short. The study proposes that 41P could be undergoing an unusually intense phase of activity or could be the remnant of a much larger body that fragmented in the past.
