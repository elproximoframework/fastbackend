# How Did 58 Tortillas Get into Space? The Science Behind the Artemis 2 Menu

For their 10-day journey around the Moon, the **Artemis 2** crew carries a scientifically designed menu featuring beef brisket, mac and cheese, veggie quiche, and a key staple: **58 flour tortillas**.

## The End of Crumbs in Microgravity

Tortillas have become a permanent substitute for traditional bread in NASA missions. The primary reason is **microgravity**: bread crumbs can float freely through the cabin, posing a risk to the astronauts' eyes and lungs, as well as a danger to the spacecraft's critical electronic systems.

This legacy began in 1985 with **Rodolfo Neri Vela**, the first Mexican astronaut, who requested their inclusion in the STS-61B mission. Since then, NASA has perfected the recipe, adjusting the pH of the tortillas to prevent mold formation during long-duration missions.

## The Sense of Taste in Space

The menu also includes five types of **hot sauce**. This addresses a known biological phenomenon: in microgravity, body fluids shift toward the face, causing nasal congestion similar to a head cold. This drastically reduces the sense of smell and, consequently, the perception of flavor. Strong sauces and seasonings are essential for the food to be palatable to the crew.

## A Rigorous Selection Process

The astronauts themselves—including **Christina Koch**—participated in choosing their dishes months in advance. Today's space diet seeks not only nutrition and operational safety but also the psychological well-being of the crew through familiar and diverse foods.
