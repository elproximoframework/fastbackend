# James Webb Finds Water

The James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) has detected unmistakable signatures of water vapor in the atmosphere of a distant exoplanet.

## Exoplanet K2-18b
- Located 120 light-years away.
- It is in the habitable zone of its star.
- The atmosphere contains methane and carbon dioxide in addition to water.

This discovery suggests that the planet could be an "ocean world," with a surface covered in liquid water under a hydrogen-rich atmosphere.

### Importance
It is the first time the presence of water has been confirmed on a planet of this size in the habitable zone, opening new possibilities in the search for extraterrestrial life.
