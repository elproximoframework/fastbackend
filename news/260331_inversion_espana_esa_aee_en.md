# Spain Strengthens Its Space Leadership with a 625 Million Euro Investment

The Government of Spain has approved a historic contribution of **625 million euros** dedicated to strategic programs of the **European Space Agency (ESA)**. This investment, channeled through the **Spanish Space Agency (AEE)**, consolidates Spain as ESA's fourth space power, trailing only Germany, France, and Italy.

## ESCA+: A New Perspective from Space

The largest portion of this investment, **325 million euros**, will fund the **ESCA+** program. This consists of a new constellation of three Earth observation satellites equipped with very high-resolution optics, infrared vision, and signal intelligence. These satellites will enable a more efficient response to climate crises and emergencies, in addition to strengthening national security and defense capabilities. ESCA+ will integrate with the **Atlantic Constellation** that Spain is developing alongside Portugal.

## Navigation, Connectivity, and Launchers

The remaining **300 million euros** will be distributed across three key areas:

- **Navigation (LEO-PNT):** Development of low Earth orbit satellite navigation systems, more resilient to interference and blocking.
- **Connectivity (IRIS2):** A program aimed at European sovereignty in secure communications through the deployment of satellite-based 5G and 6G networks.
- **Launchers:** Support for the new generation of private European rockets. In this field, the Alicante-based company **PLD Space** stands out with its Miura rockets.

## A Strategic Leap

Since 2018, Spain has tripled its contribution to ESA, reaching 455 million euros annually. With this new 625 million euro boost, the country not only ensures industrial return for its space sector companies but also guarantees its strategic autonomy in an infrastructure as critical as space.
