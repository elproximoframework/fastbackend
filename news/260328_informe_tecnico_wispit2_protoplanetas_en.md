# Technical Report: WISPIT 2 — Direct Observation of Two Forming Gas Giants
**Date:** March 24–25, 2026 | **Classification:** Technical disclosure | **Sources:** ESO, *The Astrophysical Journal Letters*, Phys.org, Space.com, Indian Defence Review

---

## 1. Context: The Second System of Its Kind in History

On March 24, 2026, a team led by **Chloe Lawlor** (PhD student at the Centre for Astronomy at the University of Galway, Ireland) published in ***The Astrophysical Journal Letters*** the direct spectroscopic confirmation of a second planet forming around the young star **WISPIT 2**. With this discovery, WISPIT 2 becomes **the second known system in the history of astronomy** where two planets in simultaneous formation have been directly observed. The only precedent is **PDS 70**, a T Tauri-type star located ~370 light-years away.

> *"WISPIT 2 is the best look at our own past that we have to date."*
> — **Chloe Lawlor**, Lead Author, University of Galway

---

## 2. The Star WISPIT 2 — Physical Characteristics

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Distance to Earth | **437 light-years** (constellation Aquila) |
| Stellar Mass | ~1.08 solar masses |
| Estimated Age | **~5 million years** |
| Spectral Type | Pre-main sequence star (not yet stable hydrogen fusion) |
| Protoplanetary Disk | Large, structured, with multiple concentric rings and gaps |

The extreme youth of the star is precisely its greatest scientific value: the planets are still assembling from the surrounding disk of gas and dust, offering a direct window into the processes that formed Jupiter, Saturn, and, by extension, Earth ~4.5 billion years ago.

---

## 3. The Two Confirmed Planets: WISPIT 2b and WISPIT 2c

| Parameter | WISPIT 2b | WISPIT 2c |
|---|---|---|
| Initial Detection Year | 2025 | 2026 (confirmed) |
| Mass | ~4.9 × Jupiter mass | ~9.8 × Jupiter mass (≈ 2× 2b) |
| Distance to Star | ~57 AU (~60× Earth-Sun distance) | ~14 AU (4× closer than 2b) |
| Type | Gas giant | Gas giant |
| Orbital Evidence | Prominent disk gap | Secondary gap closer to the star |
| Spectroscopic Signature | — | K-band CO absorption bands (young atmosphere) |

> **Note:** The original script indicates "5 and 10 times the mass of Jupiter." The exact data from the article are 4.9× (WISPIT 2b) and ~9.8× (WISPIT 2c), fully consistent with those rounded figures.

## 4. Instruments Used — Observational Methodology

The confirmation of WISPIT 2c required a chain of complementary instruments operating in different modes:

### 4.1 SPHERE (VLT, ESO — Cerro Paranal, Chile)
- **Function:** High-resolution, high-contrast direct imaging
- **Key technology:** Extreme adaptive optics to correct atmospheric turbulence + coronagraph to block light from the central star
- **Result:** Direct image of object WISPIT 2c, separating it from the noise of the star and the disk

### 4.2 GRAVITY+ (VLTI — VLT Interferometer)
- **Function:** High angular resolution interferometric spectroscopy
- **Key technology:** Combines light from four 8.2m VLT telescopes as if they were a single ~130m diameter mirror
- **Recent update:** GRAVITY+ is the enhanced version of GRAVITY, with new adaptive optics modules on each auxiliary telescope that extend capability for faint objects near bright stars
- **Result:** Confirmation of the planetary nature of the object (vs. background star) and detection of K-band CO absorption bands, chemical signature of a young gas giant atmosphere

> *"Critically, our study leveraged the recent upgrade to GRAVITY+, without which we would not have been able to obtain such a clear detection of the planet so close to its star."*
> — **Guillaume Bourdarot**, Max Planck Institute for Extraterrestrial Physics, Garching

### 4.3 MagAO-X (Magellan Telescopes, 6.5 m — Las Campanas, Chile)
- Provided the first hints of a second object in the system before confirmation with VLT/VLTI

### 4.4 LMIRcam / LBTI (*Large Binocular Telescope Interferometer*, Arizona)
- Complementary thermal infrared observations that helped characterize the planet's signal

## 5. Protoplanetary Disk Structure — What Sets WISPIT 2 Apart from PDS 70

The primary scientific distinction from the only comparable system (PDS 70) is the **structural complexity of the disk**:

| Feature | PDS 70 | WISPIT 2 |
|---|---|---|
| Disk Extension | Moderate, single central cavity | **Large, multiple concentric rings and gaps** |
| Confirmed Planets | 2 (in a single inner cavity) | 2 in separate gaps |
| Signs of 3rd Planet | No | **Yes** — narrower and shallower gap at a greater distance |
| Study Laboratory | Single cavity formation | Formation of a **complete planetary system** |

Gaps form because accreting planets gravitationally accumulate disk material orbiting in their paths, clearing circular bands. Each gap's width and depth allow for an estimation of the responsible planet's mass.

---

## 6. Possible Third Planet — Hints in the Disk

Beyond WISPIT 2b and 2c, the team has identified a third gap further from the star than 2b. Its characteristics are:

- **Narrower and shallower** than the gaps of 2b and 2c
- Consistent with a planet of **Saturn-like mass** (~0.3 Jupiter masses)
- Too faint to be confirmed with today's VLT

> *"We suspect there may be a third planet carving out this gap, potentially of Saturnian mass, as the gap is noticeably narrower and shallower."*
> — **Chloe Lawlor**, Lead Author

Confirmation of this possible third planet must wait for the ESO's **Extremely Large Telescope (ELT)**, currently under construction in the Atacama Desert, with first light expected in **March 2029**.

---

## 7. Implications for Understanding Solar System Formation

The pattern of WISPIT 2 —two massive gas giants with mass ranges and orbital separations similar to PDS 70— suggests the existence of a **"Goldilocks zone"** for gas giant formation: a combination of stellar mass, age, and disk profile that favors the accretion of massive planets at certain distance ranges.

If the analogy to our solar system is correct, terrestrial-type rocky planets (Earth-like) would currently be forming in the inner zones of the disk, too close to WISPIT 2 to be detected with current instruments.
