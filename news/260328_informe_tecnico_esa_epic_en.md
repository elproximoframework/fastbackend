# Technical Report: EPIC Mission — ESA Onboard SpaceX's Crew Dragon
**Date:** March 2026 | **Classification:** Technical disclosure | **Sources:** ESA, SpaceNews, European Spaceflight, Orbital Today

---

## 1. Strategic Context: The Press of the Clock

The **International Space Station (ISS)** is scheduled for decommissioning in 2030, with a controlled reentry shortly thereafter. This imposes critical time pressure on the **European Space Agency (ESA)**: of the five career astronauts selected in 2022, only two were guaranteed a flight to the station through the current barter agreement with NASA.

- **Sophie Adenot** (France): currently onboard the ISS on SpaceX's Crew-12 mission.
- **Raphaël Liégeois** (Belgium): assigned to a mission currently in planning.
- **Pablo Álvarez Fernández** (Spain), **Rosemary Coogan** (United Kingdom), and **Marco Sieber** (Switzerland): no flight assigned until the announcement of the EPIC mission.

Without an alternative solution, three astronauts from the class of 2022 would have been left without the possibility of flying to the ISS before its deorbiting, losing years of critical operational experience for future exploration programs (Artemis, commercial stations, etc.).

---

## 2. The EPIC Mission — ESA Provided Institutional Crew

On **March 19, 2026**, at the conclusion of the **345th meeting of the ESA Council** held in Interlaken (Switzerland), Director General **Josef Aschbacher** announced the member states' approval of the **EPIC** project (*ESA Provided Institutional Crew*).

### Operational Parameters

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Launch Vehicle | Falcon 9 Block 5 |
| Spacecraft | SpaceX Crew Dragon (reusable capsule) |
| Target Launch Date | **Q1 2028** |
| Mission Duration | ~30 days (mid-duration mission) |
| Crew Size | 4 professional astronauts |
| ISS Docking Port | Harmony module (zenith or forward port) |
| Reentry | Splashdown in the Atlantic or Pacific |

### Crew Composition

The mission is not exclusively European: ESA **buys launch capacity for four professional astronauts**, intending to implement it alongside **international partners**. The agency will lead and operate the mission, although the inclusion of non-ESA astronauts is contemplated:

- **Pablo Álvarez Fernández** 🇪🇸 — Military aeronautical engineer, the first Spaniard from the 2022 class.
- **Rosemary Coogan** 🇬🇧 — Astrophysicist from the University of Sussex, ESA's first post-Brexit career astronaut from the UK.
- **Marco Sieber** 🇨🇭 — Emergency medicine specialist.
- **1 International Partner Astronaut** — Country and profile yet to be confirmed.

## 3. Differences from Previous Private Missions

ESA has been explicit in distinguishing the EPIC project from short-duration private missions (such as Axiom Space Ax-3 or Ax-4), in which European astronauts have already flown:

| Feature | Private Missions (Axiom) | EPIC Mission |
|---|---|---|
| Average Duration | ~14 days | ~30 days |
| Crew Member Type | Space tourists / mixed | Professional career astronauts |
| Activities | Primarily proprietary research | Research + maintenance + logistics + station operations |
| Operational Leadership | Axiom Space | ESA |
| Comparison Benchmark | — | Similar to NASA's Crew-X |

ESA director of Human and Robotic Exploration, **Daniel Neuenschwander**, emphasized that EPIC is a full institutional mission, not a "private flight" with a European logo.

---

## 4. Vehicle: SpaceX Crew Dragon

The **Crew Dragon** (capsules *Endurance* or *Freedom*, reusable) is the only crewed vehicle certified for missions to the ISS since western partners abandoned the Russian Soyuz program following the geopolitical tensions of 2022.

### Relevant Technical Specifications

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Maximum Crew | 7 astronauts |
| Launch Mass | ~12,519 kg |
| Pressurized Interior Volume | 9.3 m³ (cabin) + 37 m³ (unpressurized trunk) |
| Maneuvering Propulsion | 16 Draco thrusters (400 N each) |
| Escape System | Integrated SuperDraco (8 engines, 71 kN each) |
| Autonomous Time in Orbit | Up to 210 days |
| Capsule Reuse | Up to ~15 flights per capsule |

The capsule docks autonomously to the **Harmony** module of the ISS using the IDA (*International Docking Adapter*) system.

## 5. Training and Status of the 2022 Class

The five astronauts completed their **one-year basic training** at the European Astronaut Centre (EAC) in Cologne, Germany, and received **official certification on April 22, 2024**. Their training included:

- ISS systems and flight operations
- Extravehicular activities (EVA) in a neutral buoyancy facility
- Robotics and robotic arm operations
- Emergency medical and survival training
- Familiarization with Soyuz and Dragon/Starliner spacecraft

After being certified, they have continued with the **pre-assignment training** phase, which includes specializations in scientific experiments, incremental training, and mission control internships.

---

## 6. Context: End of Life for the ISS and European Successor

The ISS has been continuously operational since 2000 and is scheduled to be **deorbited in 2030**, although several U.S. senators are pushing for an extension until 2032. Its reentry will be performed by a dedicated reentry vehicle developed by SpaceX (*Deorbit Vehicle*).

ESA, looking toward the post-ISS period, is participating in negotiations with operators of **commercial stations** (Axiom Station, Starlab) to ensure European access to microgravity after 2030. The EPIC mission also serves as an operational testbed in that transition scenario.
