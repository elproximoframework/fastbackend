# Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS: A 12-Billion-Year-Old Galactic Fossil

The interstellar object **3I/ATLAS**, discovered in July 2025 by the ATLAS observatory in Chile, has become one of the most fascinating specimens in modern astronomy. A new analysis of its isotopic composition and orbital dynamics suggests that this comet is between **10,000 and 12,000 million years old**, which would place it as one of the oldest objects ever detected in the solar system.

## Origin and Antiquity

According to astronomers Aster Taylor and Darryl Seligman from the University of Michigan, the longevity of 3I/ATLAS can be inferred from its extremely high velocity. Physical models indicate that the faster an interstellar object travels, the more time it has spent gravitationally interacting with stars and other massive bodies, which progressively accelerates it over eons. Based on this premise, 3I/ATLAS would have been born just a few billion years after the birth of the Milky Way.

## A Messenger from a Now-Extinct System

Given its extreme age, it is highly likely that the star system where 3I/ATLAS originated no longer exists. Its isotopic composition offers a unique window into the chemistry of the early universe. Since its detection, the comet has shown significant changes, including evaporation processes and alterations in its shape following its approach to Earth, indicating it is a dynamic body despite its millennial age.

## Importance for Science

The study of objects like 3I/ATLAS allows scientists to reconstruct the history of the galaxy. As a "galactic fossil," it provides critical data on planetary formation in the early stages of the Milky Way and on how matter travels through interstellar space across immeasurable distances and times.
