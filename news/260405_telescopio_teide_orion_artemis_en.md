# Teide's TTT3 Telescope Captures Orion Spacecraft on Its Historic Journey to the Moon

The **Teide Observatory**, located in Tenerife and managed by the Canary Islands Institute of Astrophysics (IAC), has achieved a visual milestone by capturing images of the **Orion** spacecraft on its transit to the Moon. The observation was carried out by the **TTT3** (Two-meter Twin Telescope), a high-precision robotic system operated by Light Bridges.

## A Glimpse at 65,000 Kilometers

At the moment of capture, the Orion capsule was approximately **65,000 kilometers from Earth**, just hours after successfully completing the translunar injection maneuver. At that distance, the spacecraft presented a visual magnitude of **11.5**, making it detectable by medium-sized telescopes.

However, the main technical challenge was not the brightness, but the speed. The spacecraft was moving at **10,800 kilometers per hour** (3 km/s), which required the use of TTT3's advanced tracking systems to maintain focus on the space vehicle during the brief two-minute transit recorded by the scientific team.

## Cutting-Edge Robotic Technology

The TTT3 telescope is one of the most modern instruments in the Teide complex. Its ability to operate entirely autonomously and its rapid response time make it an ideal tool for tracking fast-moving objects, from near-Earth asteroids to deep exploration missions like **Artemis II**.

These images serve not only as a visual testament to the mission's progress but also demonstrate the capability of Spanish astronomical facilities to collaborate in the tracking of large-scale international space missions.
