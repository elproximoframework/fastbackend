# Rocket Lab Expands European Presence with Mynaric Acquisition

**Rocket Lab Corporation**, a leader in launch services and space systems, has received regulatory approval from the German Federal Ministry for Economic Affairs and Energy to acquire the German company **Mynaric**. This operation marks a strategic milestone for Rocket Lab, establishing its first permanent headquarters in Europe (Munich) and strengthening its capacity to supply laser communication technology on a large scale.

## The Laser Communication Revolution

Laser communication is a transformative technology for satellite constellations, offering significantly higher data speeds, enhanced security, and more efficient spectrum usage compared to traditional radio frequency (RF). However, its mass adoption has been hindered by supply chain bottlenecks.

Rocket Lab aims to apply its high-scale manufacturing model to Mynaric's optical terminals, such as the **CONDOR Mk3**, making them available in industrial volumes and at competitive prices for commercial and government customers.

## Synergies and Strategic Contracts

Mynaric already works closely with Rocket Lab as a subcontractor on **Space Development Agency (SDA)** projects in the US. These collaborations, valued at approximately $1.3 billion, include the production of 36 satellites for the transport and tracking layers of the US defense network. The acquisition will optimize production and ensure deadline compliance for these critical contracts.

## Presence in the European Market

By maintaining Mynaric's headquarters in Munich, Rocket Lab positions itself as a key player within the European space ecosystem. This base will allow the company to more directly support German and ESA space programs, integrating local technical talent with Rocket Lab's global infrastructure.
