# Innovation in Orbit: Flexible Robotics and Space Data Centers

The space industry is undergoing an accelerated transformation with the successful testing of new in-orbit maintenance technologies and the deployment of massive data processing infrastructures. From Chinese robotics to advanced AI chips, space is being prepared as an environment for high-performance computing and technical services.

## Sustain Space: Flexible Robotic Arm

Chinese startup **Sustain Space** has successfully verified its flexible robotic arm aboard the **Xiyuan-0** satellite. This device, composed of spring-like tubes, allows for exceptional mobility for refueling, repair, and space debris removal tasks. Its ability to bend and rotate at multiple angles facilitates docking with spacecraft, significantly extending their operational lifespan.

## NVIDIA Ver Rubin: AI for Deep Space

During the GTC event, **NVIDIA** CEO Jensen Huang announced the **Ver Rubin** module. This new Artificial Intelligence chip is specifically designed to overcome the Size, Weight, and Power (SWaP) constraints of spacecraft. Optimized for radiation and low-energy environments, the Ver Rubin will allow for complex data processing directly in orbit, eliminating the need to send all raw information back to Earth.

## The Race for In-Orbit Data Centers

Several companies have begun the deployment of mega-constellations intended for data processing:

- **Blue Origin (Project Sunrise):** Has applied to the FCC for permission to launch **51,600 satellites** in sun-synchronous orbits. Its goal is to create a network of space data centers connected via optical laser links.
- **StarCloud:** Plans the launch of an **88,000-satellite** constellation for data processing in narrow Earth orbit layers, focusing on low latency and technological sovereignty in space.

This new infrastructure will not only support current telecommunications but also serve as the computational foundation for future missions to the Moon and Mars.
