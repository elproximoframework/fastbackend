# Extreme Prototype Recycling: The "Moon First" Strategy in Action

SpaceX has intensified its hardware recycling program at Starbase, marking a significant shift in the management of its prototypes. Recently, the systematic reuse of critical components from **Ship 29** and **Ship 30** has been observed for new phases of ground testing and as part of the base's support infrastructure. This move not only follows a logic of cost efficiency but is also a fundamental pillar of the company's new **"Moon First"** strategy.

## From Scrap to Infrastructure

Instead of completely dismantling vehicles that have completed their initial testing cycles, SpaceX is extracting Raptor engines, flap actuators, and avionics sections to equip new test stands. Additionally, sections of the stainless steel fuselage are being repurposed into storage tanks and structures for the expansion of the "Starfactory."

This "extreme recycling" allows engineers to analyze real wear and tear on hardware after static fire and cryogenic tests, applying those lessons directly to the **V3** assembly lines.

## Lunar Priority: The Leap Toward HLS

The "Moon First" strategy involves a reorientation of Starship resources toward NASA's **Human Landing System (HLS)** variant. By accelerating hardware rotation and the recycling of older prototypes, SpaceX can focus its manufacturing capacity on ships optimized for heavy cargo transport to the lunar surface and in-orbit propellant transfer tests.

## Sustainability and Industrial Innovation

The massive use of 304L stainless steel not only facilitates rapid manufacturing but also allows for an almost infinite recycling cycle. SpaceX has established facilities at Starbase capable of processing steel from retired prototypes to reintroduce it into the supply chain or transform it into useful infrastructure, minimizing the environmental impact of its large-scale testing.

In the future, this recycling philosophy will extend to the Moon, where Starship vessels reaching the surface could be repurposed into permanent habitats, laboratories, or fuel storage, preventing them from becoming space junk.
