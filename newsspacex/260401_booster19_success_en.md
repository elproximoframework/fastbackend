# Booster 19 Successfully Completes Cryogenic Testing at Massey's

SpaceX's Starship program has reached a critical milestone with the validation of **Booster 19 (B19)**, the first "Version 3" (V3) Super Heavy booster to complete a full testing campaign. Following the structural failure of Booster 18, all eyes were on B19 and its ability to withstand the extreme pressures demanded by the new architecture.

## A Decisive Success at Massey's

The transport of Booster 19 to the **Massey's** test site was not just a logistical move, but the start of one of the most aggressive testing campaigns to date. Unlike tests performed at the orbital launch mount, Massey's allows the vehicle to be subjected to pure structural loads using hydraulic thrust rams.

During three days of intensive operations, B19 successfully passed:
1. **Ambient Pneumatic Testing:** Initial validation of welds and plumbing at controlled pressure.
2. **Four Rounds of Cryogenic Loading:** Full tank loading with supercooled liquid oxygen and nitrogen.
3. **Thrust Simulation:** Hydraulic rams applied thousands of tons of force to the rocket's base, mimicking the thrust of 33 Raptor 3 engines during liftoff.

## V3 Engineering: More Volume, Less Weight

The highlight of these tests was the visual observation of the **frost pattern**. In previous versions (V1 and V2), frost was often irregular due to internal structures. On B19, the ice formation was a perfect and uniform column from the base to the hot-staging section. This confirms that SpaceX has successfully redesigned the interior to maximize propellant volume, eliminating dead spaces and optimizing every inch of the fuselage.

Furthermore, B19's success validates that the new, lighter, and simplified manufacturing processes can withstand flight. This vehicle is designed to be the core of **Flight 12 (IFT-12)**, operating under the power of the new Raptor 3 engines.

## Next Steps

Following the success at Massey's, Booster 19 has returned to Starbase's **Mega Bay 1**. There, teams will proceed with the installation of its 33 Raptor 3 engines. Once assembly is complete, the vehicle will head to the new **Pad 2** for static fire testing before its orbital debut.
