# Starbase Metropolis: The Gigabay Grows, Massey's Reinvents Itself, and Production Accelerates

While media attention focuses on static fires and launch dates, Starbase is experiencing a silent but absolutely monumental industrial transformation. The latest activity data from the site reveals an organization that has moved past "move fast and break things" to embrace an engineering maturity that would be recognizable in any major aerospace plant in the world, but at a speed no other organization has ever achieved.

## Massey's: 285 Days of Pause and a Radical Renovation

The **Massey's Outpost** test site was practically idle for 285 days following the explosion of Ship 36 in June 2025. The reconstruction, however, was not limited to repairing damage: SpaceX took the opportunity to fundamentally redesign the safety and test support systems. The most striking improvement is the new **nitrogen purge system**: pipes that circulate liquid nitrogen through heat exchangers, generating a gas flow that prevents methane from accumulating under the stand when engines are cooling down or shutting off. It is a direct response to the unplanned ignition incidents that have affected the program. Additionally, the water deluge system has been completely reconfigured, with new discharge points and a reorganization of oxygen and nitrogen tanks to optimize flow during static fires.

The **Super Crusher**—the structural actuator system that subjects the rear section of the boosters to extreme torsion, bending, and compression forces—is operating at full capacity, simulating the loads that the grid fin attachment points will experience during actual flight. Its data determines whether Booster 20, waiting in line, needs internal reinforcement before being sealed.

## The Gigabay: From Five Levels to Industrial Colossus

Starbase's **Gigabay** has reached the fourth of its five planned levels and is already receiving its exterior cladding at the rear, protecting it from the salty and corrosive environment of the Gulf of Mexico. When completed, this structure will be able to simultaneously house up to **24 Starship vehicles** in various stages of integration and refurbishment, transforming interplanetary rocket production into something more akin to a mass-production line than the technological craftsmanship of the early prototypes.

The physical connection between the **Star Factory** and the Gigabay is advancing with the installation of large-format glass panels, a process that the fan community has dubbed "Space Tetris" due to the millimeter precision required. In the nose cone assembly line, nose cones for Ships 41, 42, 43, 45, 46, and 47 can be seen simultaneously in various stages of manufacturing. Ship 40, meanwhile, already has rear flaps, covered vents, almost the entire heat shield installed, and—a Block 3 novelty—forward flaps already mounted at a very early stage of assembly, reducing total integration time.
