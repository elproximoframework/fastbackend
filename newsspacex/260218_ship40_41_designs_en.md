# Starship Evolution: New Designs for Ship 40 and 41

SpaceX continues to accelerate the development of its interplanetary transport system with the introduction of significant design changes in **Ship 40** and **Ship 41**. These units, part of the transition to the **V3 (Block 3)** architecture, feature key structural and aerodynamic modifications aimed at improving reliability during reentry and optimizing payload capacity.

## Radical Redesign of Forward Flaps

The most visible change in the new Ship 40 and 41 is the relocation and redesign of the **forward flaps**. Based on data from previous test flights, SpaceX has implemented the following improvements:
- **Leeward Positioning:** The flaps have been shifted significantly toward the "leeward" side (away from the main heat shield) to protect the actuators from extreme heat during reentry.
- **Optimized Geometry:** They are notably smaller, thinner, and have a more aerodynamic swept shape, reducing static loads at hypersonic speeds.
- **Noseward Displacement:** They have been moved further toward the tip of the ship to improve attitude control and stability.

## New Payload Section and Heat Shield

The payload section has been completely redesigned to facilitate the deployment of large Starlink satellites and future operational missions. This new compartment integrates more robust and lightweight opening mechanisms.

Additionally, the **heat shield** on Ship 40 and 41 features a new generation of ceramic tiles and an improved installation pattern that reduces the probability of shedding and facilitates maintenance between flights. These ships are also prepared for the first **in-orbit propellant transfer** tests, featuring docking adapters integrated into their structure.

## Toward Full Operational Readiness

With the integration of **Raptor 3** engines and these aerodynamic optimizations, Ship 40 and 41 represent a qualitative leap toward a vessel capable of performing complex long-duration missions and massive space infrastructure deployments.
