# SpaceX Acquires Hexagon Masterworks to Boost Starship Tank Technology

In a strategic move to strengthen its supply chain and deepen vertical integration, SpaceX has completed the acquisition of **Hexagon Masterworks**, the aerospace division of the Norwegian company Hexagon Purus. The deal, valued at approximately **$15 million**, allows SpaceX to take full control of a critical technology: Type 4 Composite Overwrapped Pressure Vessels (COPVs).

## Technological Shielding: COPV Tanks

**COPVs (Composite Overwrapped Pressure Vessels)** are vital components in the architecture of modern rockets like Falcon 9 and Starship. They are used to store helium and nitrogen at extremely high pressures (up to 700 bar) while maintaining minimal weight. These gases are essential for:
- **Tank Pressurization:** Maintaining the structural integrity of propellant tanks as they are depleted.
- **Cold Gas Thrusters:** Controlling the ship's orientation in the vacuum of space.

By bringing this manufacturing in-house, SpaceX eliminates reliance on external suppliers, reduces costs, and can iterate on tank design with the same speed it applies to the rest of the Starship fuselage.

## Aerospace Focus and Hydrogen Exclusion

Notably, the acquisition focused exclusively on the aerospace division. Hexagon Purus will retain its hydrogen storage solutions business, a sector SpaceX has decided not to integrate for now, focusing instead on the gases required for Starship's methalox propulsion.

This purchase comes at a time when SpaceX is massively expanding its capabilities at Starbase, Texas, including the construction of its own Air Separation Unit (ASU). The combination of on-site gas production and in-house tank manufacturing positions the company as one of the most self-sufficient industrial entities on the planet.

## Starship V3 Milestones

The integration of Hexagon Masterworks technology is key to the development of the **Starship V3 (Block 3)** architecture, which requires lighter and more resilient pressurization systems to withstand the increased thrust loads of Raptor 3 engines and long-duration missions to the Moon and Mars.
