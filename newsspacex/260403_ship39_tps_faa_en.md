# Ship 39: Perfect Heat Shield and FAA Flight Exclusion Mark the Way to Flight 12

**Ship 39**, the first Starship spacecraft of the Block 3 (V3) generation, has completed its cryogenic testing campaign at the Massey's site and has returned to Starbase's Mega Bay 2 for engine installation. Its presence in the Flight 12 program is the clearest example of how far SpaceX has matured as an engineering organization.

## An Unprecedented Heat Shield

The most striking feature of Ship 39 when examining images is its heat shield. Previous Block 2 ships had areas deliberately devoid of tiles or patched with experimental configurations to accommodate instrumentation. Ship 39 features a **uniform and practically 99% complete** shield, with only a few gaps on the forward flaps to house load sensors during structural testing at Massey's. Those positions will be filled before flight.

The change in tile attachment philosophy is equally significant: SpaceX has almost entirely eliminated the adhesive that caused so many detachment problems in previous flights. The new system uses **mechanical pin attachments**, installed by an automated station within the Star Factory that ensures precision and repeatability. Only on the tip of the nose cone is adhesive temporarily retained, and its migration to the pin system is also planned. Additionally, the ship's Starlink antenna has been relocated from its previous position below the payload bay to the nose cone itself, improving communications coverage during reentry.

## Testing at Massey's: Three Cryogenic Cycles and Compression Tests

Between February 28 and March 5, 2026, Ship 39 passed **three consecutive cryogenic tests** on the Massey's thrust stand, the first of their kind for a Block 3 vehicle. The trials included loading the redesigned propellant system and the novel **"squeeze tests"**—compression tests—that simulate the mechanical forces the ship will experience when caught by the tower's Mechazilla arms returning from a mission. Passing these tests with the heat shield already installed and the four flaps in place is an unprecedented integration milestone.

## FAA Raises Flight Exclusion Ceiling to 10,000 Feet

Perhaps the most telling clue to the imminence of the static fire is bureaucratic: the **Federal Aviation Administration (FAA)** published a notice establishing a temporary air space restriction over Massey's and Starbase between April 3 and 14, raising the exclusion ceiling to **10,000 feet of altitude** (about 3,050 meters). In all previous static fire tests of ships at Massey's, the ceiling was 5,000 feet. That the FAA is doubling that margin indicates its analysts have calculated the energy released by the six Raptor 3 engines in Block 3 configuration and decided to act with extra caution. Everything points to a static fire with all engines ignited simultaneously.

On the night of March 28, the transport of a new cryogenic testing and thrust simulation stand from the Sanchez site to Massey's was observed: a massive four-legged structure designed to withstand the loads of the ship's static fire.
