# FTS Explosives Arrive: Starship Flight 12 is Now Imminent

In the world of space launches, there is an indicator that never lies about the imminence of a flight: the arrival of the **Flight Termination System (FTS)** explosives. On March 25, 2026, several local witnesses on Boca Chica Road documented the arrival of a truck classified as hazardous cargo transport—white, pristine—bound for the Starbase facilities. Industry sources confirmed that the cargo was indeed the explosives designed for the flight termination system of Starship **Flight 12**.

## What is the FTS and Why Does Its Arrival Matter So Much?

The Flight Termination System is the safety system that allows flight controllers and the FAA to destroy the vehicle in milliseconds if it deviates from its programmed trajectory and poses a threat to inhabited areas or air traffic. FTS explosives are only installed in the final weeks before a launch, once all vehicle preparation is practically finished. Their arrival is not just a technical milestone; it is the unequivocal confirmation that SpaceX and the FAA consider the launch to be just around the corner.

This information aligns perfectly with the rest of the signals accumulated during the previous weeks: Ship 39 has completed its cryogenic testing at Massey's, Booster 19 is in the process of installing the 33 Raptor 3 engines, Pad 2 has been commissioned with the first 10-engine static fire on March 16, and the FAA has published an air restriction over Starbase valid from April 3 to 14. Additionally, the **FCC granted a communications license** for Flight 12 with operating dates between May 5 and 10, 2026, extending the estimated launch window.

## Current State: All Vectors Point to Flight

Elon Musk confirmed on April 3, 2026, in a post on X, that the next Starship flight—the first of the V3 architecture—is **4 to 6 weeks away**, placing the realistic window between late April and mid-May. The main variable remains the result of the full 33-engine static fire of Booster 19: if it occurs without anomalies requiring hardware redesign, the schedule holds. If structural or performance issues are detected, the correction process could extend the timelines.

The full chain of necessary preparations before flight includes the static fire of Ship 39 at Massey's (window April 3-14), the return of the ship to Mega Bay 2 to install its own FTS explosives, the 33-engine static fire of Booster 19 (estimated between April 14 and 21), the stacking of the assembly on Pad 2, and obtaining the final launch license from the FAA.
