# Europe Injects Millions into Space Innovation: Autophagous Engines and More

**Date:** 2026-04-11

The European Commission and the European Parliament have distributed the first 5 million euros among five disruptive innovation projects for space access, with a promise to inject an additional 20 million by 2026. This initiative seeks to reduce dependence on foreign powers and foster aerospace talent on the continent.

Among the winning projects are technologies that sound like science fiction: SENER is developing an autonomous flight termination system, ArianeGroup is working on oxygen-resistant alloys, and Pangea Aerospace is advancing with its reusable aerospike engine. However, the most striking project is SHARPNESS from Alpha Impulsion, an "autophagous" engine that consumes its own fuselage as fuel to avoid generating space debris.

This capital injection proves that Europe possesses the talent necessary to compete in the new space race. The challenge now is to maintain funding and political will so that these innovations move from prototypes to operational launchers that guarantee European space sovereignty.
