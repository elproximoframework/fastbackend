# German Spectrum Rocket Stays on Ground: Last-Minute Leak

**Date:** 2026-04-11

The German Spectrum rocket, from the company Isar Aerospace, had to cancel its launch in Norway literally one hour before the countdown reached zero. The official reason given was a "suspected leak" in a composite overwrapped pressure vessel (COPV).

The mission, optimistically named "Up and Away," carried five small satellites and a scientific experiment. Isar Aerospace is competing directly with projects like Spain's PLD Space Miura 5 to be the first private European rocket to reach orbit.

While the cancellation is a temporary setback, engineers consider it the correct decision. Space is unforgiving, and following the loss of a previous rocket just 30 seconds after liftoff, Isar Aerospace prefers caution and data analysis over risking another in-flight failure.
