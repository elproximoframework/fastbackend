# China Deploys GuoWang Megaconstellation to Compete with Starlink

**Date:** 2026-04-11

China continues to accelerate the deployment of its state-run response to Starlink: the GuoWang megaconstellation. Recently, a Long March 6A rocket lifted off from Taiyuan to put another five satellites into orbit, bringing the operational total to 168 satellites at an altitude of 1,100 kilometers.

China's logistical plan is ambitious and unprecedented. They aim to launch 310 satellites this year, 900 next year, and aspire to a rate of 3,600 annual launches starting in 2028, with a target of 13,000 satellites by the 2030s. These satellites use advanced electric propulsion and large solar panels.

The highlight of this latest launch was the agility of the Chinese space program. despite detecting a gas venting problem in the second stage before liftoff, engineers managed to solve it in less than an hour. This rapid response capability on the pad is what allows China to consolidate its dominance in space access.
