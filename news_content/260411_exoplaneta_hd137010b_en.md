# HD 137010 b: "Cold Earth" Found 146 Light Years Away

**Date:** 2026-04-11

NASA has confirmed the discovery of a new rocky exoplanet named HD 137010 b, after reviewing data from the Kepler telescope's K2 mission. Located 146 light years away, this world is only 6% larger than Earth and orbits a star very similar to our Sun, though slightly cooler.

Unlike many exoplanets that orbit unstable red dwarfs, HD 137010 b resides in a relatively quiet system. However, its cooler star makes it a "frozen Earth," with estimated average temperatures around 70 degrees below zero. Scientists describe it as a cosmic "super snowball."

Despite the freezing temperatures, the planet is a fascinating candidate for astrobiology. It could harbor underground oceans if it has geothermal activity, or even liquid surface water if it possesses a dense atmosphere capable of generating a powerful greenhouse effect. ESA's CHEOPS telescope will be the next to target this world to decipher its atmospheric secrets.
