# Artemis II Mission: Historic Success and Homecoming!

**Date:** 2026-04-11

They are back home! They have survived the vacuum, radiation, a heart-pounding communications blackout on the far side of the Moon, and a descent wrapped in three-thousand-degree plasma. The Artemis II mission has ended, and the success has been, quite simply, HISTORIC.

Fifty-four years after Apollo 17, humans broke the chains of Earth's gravity once again on April 1st. The colossal SLS rocket headed for the stars with the Orion spacecraft, named *Integrity*. The crew—Reid Wiseman, Victor Glover, Christina Koch, and Jeremy Hansen—completed ten critical days of space exploration.

During the mission, they performed vital proximity operations for future dockings and set a historical distance record for a crewed mission: over 406,000 kilometers. Despite minor setbacks like a toilet blockage, morale remained high. The return culminated in a perfect splashdown in the Pacific Ocean, validating all systems for the future Artemis III mission, which will take the first woman and next man to the lunar surface.
