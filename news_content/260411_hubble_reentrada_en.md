# The End of Hubble: NASA Warns of Uncontrolled Reentry

**Date:** 2026-04-11

NASA has published a study confirming that the legendary Hubble Space Telescope will fall to Earth in the coming years in a completely uncontrolled reentry. Originally intended to be recovered by the now-retired Space Shuttle, the telescope now represents a multi-ton risk orbiting without control.

It is estimated that Hubble's orbit will fully degrade between 2029 and 2033. Because it was not designed to disintegrate safely in the atmosphere, large pieces could reach the Earth's surface. NASA estimates the risk of casualties at 1 in 330, a figure significantly higher than the standard safety limit of 1 in 10,000.

The worst-case scenario proposed by experts is the fall of debris over densely populated areas, such as Macau in southern China. After 1.7 million observations that revolutionized our understanding of the universe, Hubble faces an uncertain end, unless a last-minute space tug project emerges to ensure its safe descent.
