# Chinese Tianlong-3 Rocket Failure: Gravity is Not Optional

**Date:** 2026-04-11

The Chinese commercial rocket Tianlong-3, from the company Space Pioneer, suffered a total failure during its first test flight in the Dongfeng area. The vehicle experienced what agencies elegantly call an "anomaly after liftoff," crashing spectacularly after a brief flight.

This rocket is no minor project; with a 3.8-meter diameter and 590 tons of liftoff weight, it is essentially a clone in performance of SpaceX's Falcon 9, capable of putting 17 tons into low Earth orbit. However, Chinese space engineering seems to be moving at a pace that sometimes sacrifices quality control.

This crash recalls a previous incident months ago, when another rocket of the same name broke loose from its moorings during a static fire test and flew off uncontrollably. While Space Pioneer's development pace is impressive, these failures demonstrate that the path to orbital reliability is still long for the private Chinese company.
